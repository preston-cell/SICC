# South Dakota

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- **No state income tax**
- Common law property state (but opt-in community property trust available)
- **Premier trust jurisdiction** - dynasty trusts, asset protection
- **No rule against perpetuities** (perpetual trusts)
- Domestic Asset Protection Trusts (DAPTs)
- Unlimited homestead value (up to 160 acres rural)

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: South Dakota Codified Laws Chapter 29A-2 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (SDCL § 29A-2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 competent witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (SDCL § 29A-2-504)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in South Dakota
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be valid if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign within reasonable time of witnessing
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: SDCL Chapter 55 (Fiduciaries and Trusts)

### Trust Creation (SDCL § 55-1)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (SDCL § 55-1B)
- South Dakota allows directed trusts (pioneered concept)
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Trust protector provisions available
- Investment direction/distribution direction separation

### Trust Protector Provisions
- South Dakota pioneered trust protector legislation
- Broad powers available
- Can modify trusts, change situs, replace trustees
- Flexible planning tool

## Dynasty Trusts (PREMIER FEATURE)
**SDCL § 43-5-8**

### No Rule Against Perpetuities
- **Trusts can last forever** (perpetual)
- No termination requirement
- Multi-generational wealth transfer
- Among most favorable in nation

### Benefits
- Avoid estate tax at each generation
- Asset protection for descendants
- Family governance across generations
- Flexibility through trust protector

### Planning Opportunity
- Consider South Dakota situs for dynasty trusts
- Even for non-residents
- No state income tax on trust income
- Premier trust jurisdiction

## Domestic Asset Protection Trusts (DAPT)
**SDCL § 55-16**

### Features
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts allowed
- Strong creditor protection
- **2-year fraudulent transfer lookback** (shorter than most)
- Settlor can be discretionary beneficiary

### Requirements
- Must have South Dakota trustee
- Trust must be irrevocable
- Spendthrift clause required
- Proper funding and administration

### Protection Strength
- Among strongest DAPT jurisdictions
- 2-year lookback (some states have 4 years)
- Well-established case law
- Strong trust privacy laws

## Community Property Trust
**SDCL § 55-17**

### Opt-In Community Property
- Available since 2016
- Common law state with community property option
- Provides **double step-up in basis** at first death
- Significant tax planning opportunity

### Requirements
- Both spouses must consent
- Trust must be established
- Assets must be transferred to trust
- Must meet statutory requirements

### Benefits
- Full step-up in basis for both spouses' shares
- Can be more favorable than joint tenancy
- Valuable for highly appreciated assets
- Community property treatment

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: SDCL Chapter 59-7 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (SDCL § 59-7-105)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (SDCL § 59-7-104)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (SDCL § 59-7-301)
- South Dakota provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (SDCL § 59-7-201)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (SDCL § 59-7-120)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: SDCL Chapter 34-12D (Healthcare Decisions)

### Types Available
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Living Will
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements (SDCL § 34-12D-2)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Notarization not required but recommended
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Attending physician
  - Healthcare provider employee

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### South Dakota POST
- Available in South Dakota
- Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (SDCL § 34-12D-14)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Any adult relative

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state income tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Tax-Friendly Environment
- Triple tax advantage (no estate, inheritance, or income tax)
- Trust income also not taxed by state
- Major reason for trust situs election
- Popular domicile for wealthy individuals

### Planning Implications
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates
- Consider South Dakota trust situs for tax efficiency
- Dynasty trusts avoid state income tax
- No state-level death tax planning needed

## Medicaid Planning (South Dakota Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### South Dakota-Specific Programs
- **SD Medicaid** administered by DSS
- **Home and Community Based Services** waiver
- Estate recovery program active (but homestead protected during spouse's lifetime)

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $6,000-7,000/month (verify current rate - lower cost state)

## Homestead Protection
**SDCL § 43-31**

### Exemption Amount
- **Unlimited value** for homestead
- Up to **1 acre** in platted city or town
- Up to **160 acres** outside platted areas (rural)
- Constitutional and statutory protection

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)
- Declaration recommended for clarity

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens (with limitations)
  - Purchase money obligations

### Agricultural Protection
- 160-acre rural exemption protects family farms
- Unlimited value important for agricultural land
- Similar to Texas, Florida, Oklahoma

## Transfer on Death Deed
**SDCL § 29A-6-401 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Uniform Real Property Transfer on Death Act

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with register of deeds
- Must designate beneficiary

### Advantages
- Avoids probate for real property
- Simple and cost-effective
- Step-up in basis at death
- Revocable during lifetime

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for South Dakota real property

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (SDCL § 29A-3-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by register
- Uniform Probate Code procedures

### Formal Probate (SDCL § 29A-3-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (SDCL § 29A-3-1201)
- **Summary administration** for estates under **$50,000**
- Simplified procedures
- Affidavit procedure for smaller amounts

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Circuit Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from circuit court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement

### Trust-Friendly State
- South Dakota is major trust jurisdiction
- Trust documents and records often stored in safe deposit boxes
- Consider accessibility for out-of-state trustees
- Professional trustees may need access provisions

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Trust administration documents should be accessible to trustees
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform personal representative of box location
