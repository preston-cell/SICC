# Maine

## Key Differences from Other States
- **State estate tax** with $6.8M exemption (2025)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- Lowest top estate tax rate among estate tax states (12%)
- Exemption indexed for inflation
- Uniform Probate Code adopted
- Strong homestead protections for elderly

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Maine Revised Statutes Title 18-C (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (18-C M.R.S. § 2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (18-C M.R.S. § 2-504)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (18-C M.R.S. § 2-502)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Foreign Wills
- Wills valid where executed are valid in Maine
- Must meet Maine or execution state formalities

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: 18-C M.R.S. Article 7 (Maine Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (18-C M.R.S. § 7-402)
- Maine Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Maine UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (18-C M.R.S. § 7-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- Maine allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: 18-C M.R.S. Article 5 (Maine Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (18-C M.R.S. § 5-905)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (18-C M.R.S. § 5-904)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (18-C M.R.S. § 5-941)
- Maine provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (18-C M.R.S. § 5-920)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: 18-C M.R.S. § 5-801 et seq.

### Types Available
- Advance Health Care Directive (combined form)
- Individual Instruction (Living Will)
- Power of Attorney for Health Care

### Execution Requirements (18-C M.R.S. § 5-802)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** (or notarized)
- One witness cannot be:
  - Healthcare provider
  - Employee of healthcare provider

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Maine POLST
- Available in Maine
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (18-C M.R.S. § 5-805)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Other relative or close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Maine Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $6,800,000 (2025, indexed for inflation)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 12% (lowest among estate tax states)

### Rate Structure (36 M.R.S. § 4102)
| Taxable Estate | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $6M | 8% |
| $6M - $9M | 10% |
| Over $9M | 12% |

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Maine Revenue Services
- Must file if gross estate exceeds exemption

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- Higher exemption ($6.8M) means fewer estates affected
- Lower rates (12% max) than most estate tax states
- No portability - credit shelter trusts still valuable
- Indexed exemption provides inflation protection

## Medicaid Planning (MaineCare)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$10,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Maine-Specific Programs
- **MaineCare** is Maine's Medicaid program
- **Home and Community Based Services** waivers available
- Higher individual asset limit ($10,000) more generous
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $10,000-11,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**14 M.R.S. § 4422**

### Exemption Amount
- Standard: Up to **$80,000** equity protected
- **Age 60+ and disabled**: Up to **$160,000** protected
- Applies to dwelling and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Enhanced Protection for Elderly
- Double exemption for those 60+
- Double exemption for disabled
- Significant planning opportunity

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (18-C M.R.S. § 3-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by register
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (18-C M.R.S. § 3-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (18-C M.R.S. § 3-1201)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$40,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Administration
- For small estates with real property
- Simplified court process
- Faster than full administration

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Court** handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Low Estate Tax Rates
- 12% top rate is lowest among estate tax states
- Less aggressive than MA, WA, or HI
- Still requires planning for large estates

### Indexed Exemption
- $6.8M exemption adjusted for inflation
- Provides ongoing protection
- Higher than many estate tax states

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $10,000 individual limit more generous than most states
- Provides more flexibility in planning

### Seasonal Residents
- Many out-of-state residents have Maine property
- May trigger Maine estate tax on Maine real property
- Domicile planning important
- Consider trust ownership of vacation property

### Planning Considerations
- Higher exemption and lower rates reduce urgency
- Credit shelter trusts still valuable (no portability)
- Consider domicile if choosing between ME and no-tax state
- Account for seasonal property ownership
- Higher Medicaid asset limit provides flexibility
- Enhanced homestead for elderly valuable

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative appointment needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement

### Estate Tax Considerations
- Maine has estate tax ($6.8M threshold)
- Document valuable contents for tax purposes
- Lower rates reduce but don't eliminate tax impact

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document contents for estate tax reporting
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider naming PR as joint renter for easier access
