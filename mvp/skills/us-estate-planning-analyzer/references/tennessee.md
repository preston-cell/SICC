# Tennessee

## Key Differences from Other States
- **No state estate tax** (repealed January 1, 2016)
- **No state inheritance tax** (repealed 2016)
- **No state income tax** (Hall Tax repealed 2021)
- Common law property state (but opt-in community property trust available)
- Strong asset protection trust laws
- Holographic wills recognized
- Very limited homestead protection ($5,000)
- Community property trust available

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Tennessee Code Annotated Title 32

### Execution Requirements (T.C.A. § 32-1-104)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by mark/another at direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 competent witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (T.C.A. § 32-1-104)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (T.C.A. § 32-1-105)
- **Recognized** in Tennessee
- Must be **entirely in testator's handwriting**
- Must be **signed by testator**
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Nuncupative (Oral) Wills (T.C.A. § 32-1-106)
- Recognized in limited circumstances
- Only for personal property under $1,000
- Must be made during last sickness
- Must have 2 witnesses

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: T.C.A. Title 35 (Tennessee Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (T.C.A. § 35-15-402)
- Tennessee Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Tennessee UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (T.C.A. § 35-15-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (T.C.A. § 35-15-808)
- Tennessee allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Investment direction/distribution direction separation

### Trust Decanting (T.C.A. § 35-15-816)
- Authorized under Tennessee UTC
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Community Property Trust
**T.C.A. § 35-17 (Tennessee Community Property Trust Act of 2010)**

### Opt-In Community Property
- Available since 2010 (pioneered by Tennessee)
- Common law state with community property option
- Provides **double step-up in basis** at first death
- Significant tax planning opportunity

### Requirements
- Both spouses must consent
- Trust must be established meeting statutory requirements
- Assets must be transferred to trust
- Must be properly documented

### Benefits
- Full step-up in basis for both spouses' shares at first death
- Can be more favorable than joint tenancy
- Valuable for highly appreciated assets
- Community property treatment without changing domicile

## Asset Protection Trusts
**T.C.A. § 35-16 (Tennessee Investment Services Trust Act)**

### Features
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts allowed
- Qualified disposition in trust
- **4-year fraudulent transfer lookback** (standard)
- Settlor can be discretionary beneficiary

### Requirements
- Must have Tennessee trustee
- Trust must be irrevocable
- Spendthrift clause required
- Proper funding and administration
- Must be "qualified disposition"

### Protection Strength
- Established asset protection jurisdiction
- 4-year lookback period
- Creditor protection for settlor
- No state income tax advantage

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: T.C.A. Title 34, Chapter 6 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (T.C.A. § 34-6-104)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (T.C.A. § 34-6-103)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (T.C.A. § 34-6-109)
- Tennessee provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (T.C.A. § 34-6-108)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (T.C.A. § 34-6-115)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: T.C.A. Title 68, Chapter 11 (Tennessee Right to Natural Death Act)

### Types Available
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Living Will (Advance Directive)
- Combined Advance Care Plan

### Execution Requirements
**Healthcare POA (T.C.A. § 34-6-201)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Agent cannot be witness

**Living Will (T.C.A. § 68-11-1803)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Physician
  - Healthcare provider employee
  - Related to declarant

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Tennessee POST
- Available in Tennessee
- Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Bright green form

### Default Surrogate (T.C.A. § 68-11-1806)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Any adult relative
  - Responsible adult

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (repealed January 1, 2016)
- **No state inheritance tax** (repealed 2016)
- **No state income tax** (Hall Tax repealed January 1, 2021)
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Tax-Friendly Environment
- Triple tax advantage (no estate, inheritance, or income tax)
- Very attractive for wealthy individuals and retirees
- No state-level death tax planning needed
- Focus on federal planning for large estates

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Community property trust for basis step-up
- Asset protection trusts available
- Probate avoidance for privacy and efficiency
- Consider Tennessee domicile for tax planning

## Medicaid Planning (TennCare)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Tennessee-Specific Programs
- **TennCare** is Tennessee's Medicaid program
- **CHOICES** program for home and community-based services
- **ECF CHOICES** for employment and community first
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $6,000-7,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**T.C.A. § 26-2-301**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$5,000** for individuals (very limited)
- Up to **$7,500** for joint owners
- **One of lowest protections in nation**
- Applies to dwelling and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Limited Protection
- **Very low exemption** - consider alternative strategies
- Trust ownership for asset protection
- Tennessee DAPT for creditor protection
- LLC/entity ownership for real estate

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not specifically authorized** by statute in Tennessee
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Regular Administration (T.C.A. Title 30)
- Probate court handles estates
- Personal representative appointed
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Small Estate Procedures (T.C.A. § 30-4-103)
- **Summary administration** for estates under **$50,000**
- Simplified procedures
- Expedited process

### Affidavit Procedure (T.C.A. § 30-4-103)
- For small personal property claims
- 45-day waiting period after death
- No real property included

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from publication

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Court** (or Chancery Court) handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### No State Taxes (Triple Advantage)
- No state estate tax (since 2016)
- No state inheritance tax (since 2016)
- No state income tax (since 2021)
- Very tax-friendly domicile

### Community Property Trust
- Pioneered community property trust (2010)
- Double step-up in basis available
- Valuable for appreciated assets
- Common law state with opt-in option

### Asset Protection Trusts
- Tennessee Investment Services Trust Act
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts
- 4-year fraudulent transfer lookback
- Creditor protection available

### Very Limited Homestead
- $5,000 individual / $7,500 joint
- Among lowest in nation
- Requires alternative asset protection strategies
- Trust and entity planning critical

### Planning Considerations
- Tax-friendly domicile for wealthy individuals
- Community property trust for step-up in basis
- **Very limited homestead** - use trusts for asset protection
- Consider Tennessee trust situs for asset protection
- Holographic wills recognized (but formal will preferred)
- No TOD deed - use trusts for real property

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Tennessee allows examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- No state estate or inheritance tax simplifies planning
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor of box location

## State Charitable Incentives

### Limited State Credits
- Tennessee has no state income tax
- No state-level charitable deductions or credits
- Federal charitable planning applies

### Hall Tax Repeal
- Hall Tax (on investment income) repealed 2021
- Previously had some charitable implications
- No longer relevant

### Estate Planning Implications
- Federal charitable deductions available
- No state tax benefit for charitable giving
- Community property trust can include charitable provisions
- Private foundation administration (no state income tax)
