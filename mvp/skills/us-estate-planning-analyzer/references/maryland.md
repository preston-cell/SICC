# Maryland

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has BOTH state estate tax AND inheritance tax** (only state with both)
- Estate tax exemption $5M (not indexed)
- Inheritance tax 10% flat rate on non-exempt beneficiaries
- Close relatives exempt from inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- Combined tax burden can be significant
- Orphans' Court handles probate

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Maryland Estates and Trusts Article

### Execution Requirements (Md. Est. & Trusts § 4-102)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be **signed by 2 credible witnesses** in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Md. Est. & Trusts § 4-102)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Maryland
- All wills must be witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills recognized if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be credible witnesses
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Must sign in presence of testator

### Military Wills
- Special provisions for military personnel
- Reduced formalities in some circumstances

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Md. Est. & Trusts Title 14 (Maryland Trust Act)

### Trust Creation (Md. Est. & Trusts § 14-401)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Maryland Trust Act governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon death

### Revocable Trusts (Md. Est. & Trusts § 14-403)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- Maryland allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following proper directions

### Trust Decanting
- Maryland allows trust decanting
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretion required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Md. Est. & Trusts § 17-101 et seq.

### Execution Requirements (Md. Est. & Trusts § 17-102)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- All three required

### Durability (Md. Est. & Trusts § 17-103)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (Md. Est. & Trusts § 17-202)
- Maryland provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Delegate authority

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Md. Health-Gen. § 5-601 et seq.

### Types Available
- Advance Directive (combined form)
- Living Will
- Healthcare Agent Appointment

### Execution Requirements (Md. Health-Gen. § 5-602)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Related by blood or marriage
  - Entitled to any portion of estate
  - Financially responsible for healthcare

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Maryland MOLST
- Medical Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment
- Available for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (Md. Health-Gen. § 5-605)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Friend or other relative

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Maryland Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $5,000,000 (not indexed)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Rate**: 16% flat rate on taxable estate above exemption

### Maryland Inheritance Tax
- **State inheritance tax ALSO applies**
- **Rate**: 10% flat rate
- Tax based on relationship to decedent

### Exempt Beneficiaries (No Inheritance Tax)
- Surviving spouse
- Parents
- Grandparents
- Children (including stepchildren and adopted)
- Other lineal descendants
- Siblings
- Spouse of child (daughter/son-in-law)

### Taxable Beneficiaries (10% Inheritance Tax)
- All other beneficiaries:
  - Nieces and nephews
  - Cousins
  - Friends
  - Unmarried partners
  - Charities (some exemptions apply)

### Interaction Between Taxes
- Estate tax reduced by inheritance tax paid
- Not taxed twice on same transfer
- Credit given for inheritance tax against estate tax

### Combined Tax Example
$6M estate passing to friend:
- Estate tax: 16% on $1M over exemption = $160,000
- Inheritance tax: 10% on $6M = $600,000
- Credit for inheritance tax reduces estate tax
- Net combined tax substantial

### Filing Requirements
- Estate tax return due 9 months after death
- Inheritance tax return due 9 months after death
- File with Comptroller of Maryland
- Extensions available

### Planning Implications
- **Only state with BOTH taxes** - comprehensive planning essential
- Structure bequests to exempt beneficiaries when possible
- Credit shelter trusts critical (no portability)
- ILIT for life insurance to avoid both taxes
- Consider lifetime gifts to non-exempt beneficiaries
- Charitable giving reduces both tax bases

## Medicaid Planning (Maryland Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,500
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Maryland-Specific Programs
- **Maryland Medicaid** administered by Maryland Department of Health
- **Community First Choice** program
- **Home and Community Based Waivers**
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $10,000-11,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Maryland Courts and Judicial Proceedings § 11-504**

### Exemption Amount
- **No general homestead exemption**
- Limited bankruptcy exemption only
- Tenancy by entireties provides some protection

### Tenancy by Entireties
- Property held by married couples
- Protected from individual creditors of one spouse
- Primary form of real estate protection in Maryland
- Automatic for married couples owning jointly

### Alternatives
- Consider trust ownership
- Life insurance for asset replacement
- Retirement account protections

## Probate Procedures

### Regular Estate (Md. Est. & Trusts § 5-301)
- Court supervised administration
- Personal representative appointed by Register of Wills
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Small Estate (Md. Est. & Trusts § 5-601)
- **Modified administration** for estates under **$50,000**
- Simplified procedures
- Less court involvement
- **No administration** for estates under **$50,000** to surviving spouse
- **$100,000** threshold for surviving spouse/minor children

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months from appointment

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Orphans' Court** handles probate (unique to Maryland)
- **Register of Wills** administers estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Both Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **Only state requiring planning for BOTH taxes**
- Comprehensive planning essential
- Consider all beneficiary relationships
- Structure bequests strategically

### Orphans' Court
- Unique probate court system
- Elected judges in most counties
- Register of Wills handles administration

### No Homestead Protection
- One of few states without general homestead exemption
- Tenancy by entireties is primary protection
- Trust planning more important

### Planning Considerations
- **Both taxes apply** - identify all beneficiary classes
- Structure bequests to exempt beneficiaries when possible
- Credit shelter trusts critical (no portability)
- ILIT for life insurance to avoid both taxes
- Consider lifetime gifts to non-exempt beneficiaries
- Charitable bequests reduce both tax bases
- Life insurance to charity avoids both taxes
- Use tenancy by entireties for creditor protection

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required from Orphans' Court
- Bank may permit limited access with death certificate to locate will only
- Register of Wills may authorize examination
- Full access requires letters of administration

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze at death
- Contents accessible without Orphans' Court involvement
- May simplify access but consider estate tax implications

### Inventory Requirements
- Orphans' Court may require inventory of contents
- Important for inheritance tax reporting on non-exempt beneficiaries
- Document valuable items

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document contents for estate AND inheritance tax reporting
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider naming PR as joint renter

## State Charitable Incentives

### Endow Maryland Tax Credit
- 25% state tax credit for donations to qualified permanent endowments
- Maximum credit: $50,000 individual / $100,000 joint
- Applies to gifts to community foundations meeting requirements
- Very valuable for high-net-worth charitable planning

### Heritage Structure Rehabilitation Tax Credit
- Credit for rehabilitation of historic structures
- Can apply to charitable donations of preservation easements
- Coordinates with federal historic preservation incentives

### Estate Planning Implications
- Endow Maryland credit exceptionally valuable (25% rate)
- With BOTH estate and inheritance tax, charitable giving very tax-efficient
- Charitable bequests reduce estate tax base AND avoid inheritance tax
- Charitable remainder trusts provide income while reducing both taxes
- Consider frontloading charitable gifts during Maryland residency
- Life insurance to charity avoids both estate and inheritance tax
