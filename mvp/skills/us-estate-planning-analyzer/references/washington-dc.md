# Washington D.C.

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has DC estate tax** (exemption ~$4.87M - LOWER than federal)
- No inheritance tax
- Federal district (not a state) but acts as jurisdiction
- **No portability** for DC estate tax
- Electronic wills recognized
- Small estate threshold recently increased to $80,000 (2025)
- Unlimited homestead protection in bankruptcy

## Estate Tax
**DC has its own estate tax separate from federal**

### Exemption (2025)
- **$4,873,200** per person
- Indexed annually for inflation
- **Much lower than federal** $13.99M exemption

### Tax Rates
- Graduated rates from **11.2% to 16%**

### Critical Feature - NO PORTABILITY
- DC does **NOT** allow portability between spouses
- Unused exemption permanently lost without planning
- Must use credit shelter trusts for married couples

### Planning Impact
- $8M estate: No federal tax but ~$397,242 DC estate tax
- Many DC residents miss this exposure

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: DC Code Title 18

### Execution Requirements (DC Code § 18-103)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator**
- Must be attested by at least **2 credible witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in presence of testator

### Interested Witnesses (DC Code § 18-104)
- Not disqualified from serving
- But bequest may be void unless will would have been made without their testimony

### Electronic Wills (DC Code § 18-908)
- **DC recognizes electronic wills**
- Can be signed electronically
- Witnesses may be present via live audio-video technology

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in DC

### Oral Wills (DC Code § 18-107)
- Limited recognition for personal property only
- Must be made during last illness
- Two witnesses required

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: DC Code Title 19, Chapter 13 (Uniform Trust Code)

### Features
- Uniform Trust Code adopted
- No signature requirement for living trust validity
- No mandatory registration requirement

### Certification of Trust (DC Code § 19-1310.13)
- Trustee can provide certification instead of full trust

## Power of Attorney

### Financial POA
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Must be **notarized**

### Durable POA for Health Care (DC Code §§ 21-2205, 21-2207)
- Must be **dated and signed**
- Requires **2 adult witnesses**
- Witnesses cannot include: principal, healthcare provider, financially responsible party, heirs/beneficiaries

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: DC Code § 21-2201 et seq.

### Execution Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed and dated**
- Must be signed by **2 or more witnesses** (at least 18 years old)

### Mental Capacity (DC Code § 21-2204)
- Incapacity must be determined by **2 licensed professionals**

## Medicaid Planning (DC Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years) for Nursing Home and HCBS Waivers
- Does NOT apply to ABD Medicaid

### Asset Limits (2025)
- **Individual: $4,000**
- **Married couples: $6,000**

### Income Limits (2025)
- Single: $1,305/month
- Medically needy threshold: ~$837-881/month

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence
- Household furnishings
- Personal effects
- One vehicle
- Prepaid irrevocable burial

## Homestead Protection

### Property Tax Homestead Deduction (DC Code § 47-850)
- Reduces assessed value by **$89,850**
- Annual assessment cap: **10% increase** per year

### Bankruptcy Homestead (DC Code § 15-501(a)(14))
- **Unlimited value protection** for primary home
- Applies to home, condo, co-op interest
- Much stronger than many states

## Probate Procedures

### Small Estate Threshold (DC Code § 20-351 et seq.)
- **$80,000** (increased from $40,000 in March 2025)
- Generally handled within **120 days**
- No attorney required

### Spousal Rights
- Cannot disinherit spouse or domestic partner
- **Homestead allowance: $30,000** (increased 2025)
- **Exempt property: $20,000** (increased 2025)

## Unique Features

### Federal District Status
- Not a state but acts as jurisdiction
- High concentration of federal employees
- Special considerations for federal retirement benefits

### Tri-State Planning (DC/MD/VA)
- Many DC residents own property in multiple jurisdictions
- Must coordinate multi-jurisdiction probate
- Domicile determination affects estate tax exposure

### 2026 Federal Exemption Sunset
- Federal exemption scheduled to sunset to ~$7M
- Combined with DC's $4.87M threshold, more estates will be taxable
- Review all plans before sunset
