# New York

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has state estate tax** (exemption ~$7.16M in 2025)
- **Cliff effect at 105%** of exemption
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **No portability** of estate tax exemption
- **3-year gift add-back rule**
- Strict will execution requirements
- No holographic wills (except military)
- Surrogate's Court handles probate

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: New York Estates, Powers and Trusts Law (EPTL)

### Execution Requirements (EPTL § 3-2.1)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed at end** of will by testator (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses** within 30 days
- Testator must **declare** instrument is will
- Witnesses must sign **in presence of each other**

### Self-Proving Affidavit (EPTL § 3-2.1)
- Available and strongly recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in New York
- Exception: Military personnel in armed conflict
- All civilian wills must be formally witnessed

### Strict Compliance Required
- New York courts strictly interpret will requirements
- Minor defects can invalidate will
- Professional drafting strongly recommended
- Proper execution ceremony essential

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in presence of each other
- Must sign within 30 days of testator signing
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not invalidate but may affect bequest

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: EPTL Article 7 and 7-A

### Trust Creation (EPTL § 7-1.1)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- EPTL and common law govern
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon death

### Revocable Trusts (EPTL § 7-1.16)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed irrevocable unless stated otherwise (NY default)
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (EPTL § 7-1.12)
- New York allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor/protector can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (EPTL § 10-6.6)
- Authorized under New York law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required
- Notice to beneficiaries and AG (if charitable)

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: General Obligations Law (GOL) Article 5, Title 15

### Execution Requirements (GOL § 5-1501B)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- **All three required**
- **Statutory short form** strongly recommended
- Separate **Statutory Gifts Rider** for gifts

### Durability (GOL § 5-1501A)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA NOT available in standard form

### Statutory Short Form (GOL § 5-1513)
- New York provides statutory short form (strongly recommended)
- Non-statutory forms may face acceptance issues
- Banks and institutions prefer statutory form
- Third parties generally must accept

### Statutory Gifts Rider (GOL § 5-1514)
- Required for agent to make gifts
- Must be executed separately
- Specific limitations on gifting authority
- Principal must initial specific provisions

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Third Party Acceptance (GOL § 5-1504)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- 10 business days to accept or reject
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Proxy
**Statutory Basis**: Public Health Law (PHL) Article 29-C

### Healthcare Proxy (PHL § 2981)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be healthcare agent
- Notarization not required but recommended

### Living Will
- Not governed by specific statute
- Common law recognition
- Provides guidance but not binding on agent
- Combined with healthcare proxy recommended

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### New York MOLST
- Medical Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment
- Available for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Pink form

### Default Surrogate (Family Health Care Decisions Act)
- If no healthcare proxy and patient lacks capacity:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Close friend (limited)

## Estate Tax

### New York Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $7,160,000 (2025, indexed for inflation)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%

### CLIFF EFFECT - CRITICAL
- If taxable estate exceeds **105%** of exemption (~$7.52M in 2025)
- **Exemption is completely lost**
- Entire estate taxed from dollar one
- More gradual than MA cliff but still significant

### Rate Structure
- Graduated rates from 3.06% to 16%
- Based on taxable estate amount
- Rates increase progressively

### 3-Year Gift Add-Back (Tax Law § 954)
- **Taxable gifts made within 3 years of death added back**
- Different from federal gift tax treatment
- Affects deathbed planning
- Not a gift tax - inclusion rule

### No Portability
- Cannot elect portability of deceased spouse's exemption
- Credit shelter trusts important for married couples
- Each spouse must use their own exemption

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No separate state gift tax** (but 3-year add-back rule)
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with NYS Department of Taxation and Finance
- Must file if gross estate exceeds exemption

### Planning Implications
- **Cliff effect at 105%** creates planning urgency
- 3-year gift add-back is unusual
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- ILIT for life insurance
- Consider domicile change for very large estates
- Keep estate below 105% threshold when possible

## Medicaid Planning (NY Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** for nursing home Medicaid
- **30 months** for home care (but changes proposed)
- **No look-back** for Community Medicaid (historical - subject to change)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: Approximately **$31,175** (for Community Medicaid)
- Nursing home: ~$2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $1,732/month for Medicaid-only
- Higher for dual eligible
- Pooled income trust available for excess income

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$1,071,000 - higher than federal)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### New York-Specific Programs
- **NY Medicaid** administered by LDSS
- **MLTC** (Managed Long-Term Care)
- **Pooled Income Trusts** for income above limit
- **Spousal Refusal** still available
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Spousal refusal available (unique to NY)

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $14,000-15,000/month (verify current rate - very high cost state)

## Homestead Protection
**CPLR § 5206**

### Exemption Amount
- **Varies by county** ($75,000 to $300,000)
- NYC and downstate: $300,000
- Other counties: $75,000 to $200,000
- Applies to dwelling and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Limited to one property
- Automatic protection

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not authorized** in New York
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property
- Enhanced life estate deed available in some circumstances

## Probate Procedures

### Probate (SCPA)
- **Surrogate's Court** in each county handles probate
- Executor/administrator appointed by Surrogate
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond may be required

### Small Estate Procedures (SCPA § 1301)
- **Summary administration** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- Voluntary administration affidavit
- Simplified procedures

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 9-18 months typical
- Small estate: 3-6 months
- Complex estates: 18-36 months
- Creditor claim period: 7 months from issuance of letters

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Surrogate's Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided (or owned property) has jurisdiction
- Each county has its own Surrogate

## Unique State Features

### Cliff Effect Creates Urgency
- 105% threshold means careful planning required
- Small increase can cause huge tax increase
- Keep estate below 105% of exemption

### 3-Year Gift Add-Back
- Unusual rule affecting planning
- Gifts within 3 years added to taxable estate
- Not a gift tax but inclusion rule

### Strict Will Requirements
- NY courts enforce formalities strictly
- Proper execution ceremony essential
- Professional drafting recommended

### Spousal Refusal
- Unique Medicaid planning tool
- Community spouse can refuse to contribute
- Still available but use carefully

### High Cost State
- Very high Medicaid penalty divisor
- High property values
- Complex planning often needed

### Planning Considerations
- Stay below 105% of exemption to avoid cliff
- Account for 3-year gift add-back
- Credit shelter trusts for married couples
- ILIT for life insurance
- Consider domicile change for very large estates
- Strict will execution requirements

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from surrogate's court required
- New York allows limited access with death certificate to search for will
- Executor/administrator needed for full access and removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without surrogate court involvement

### Estate Tax Considerations
- New York has estate tax with 105% cliff effect at $7.16M threshold
- Contents valued for estate tax - critical near threshold
- Document all valuable items carefully

### Special New York Rules
- EPTL governs safe deposit box access procedures
- Surrogate's court in county of residence handles administration

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document contents carefully for estate tax (cliff effect makes this critical)
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider estate tax implications for valuable items
