# Nevada

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Community property state**
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- **No state income tax**
- **Strong asset protection trust laws**
- **Dynasty trusts permitted** (365-year RAP)
- Popular for trust situs
- Strong homestead protection ($605,000)
- Holographic wills recognized

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 133

### Execution Requirements (NRS 133.040)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (NRS 133.050)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (NRS 133.090)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness does not invalidate will
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: NRS Chapter 163 and 166 (Nevada Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (NRS 163.002)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Nevada trust law governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Nevada-friendly trustee laws

### Revocable Trusts (NRS 163.560)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (NRS 163.5549)
- Nevada allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor/protector can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Attractive for institutional trustees

## Self-Settled Spendthrift Trusts (Asset Protection)
**NRS Chapter 166**

### Nevada DAPT Features
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts allowed
- **2-year fraudulent transfer lookback** (shortest in nation)
- Must have Nevada trustee (at least one)
- Settlor can be discretionary beneficiary
- Strong asset protection

### Requirements
- Irrevocable trust
- At least one Nevada trustee
- Trust situs in Nevada
- Spendthrift provision
- Not fraudulent transfer

### Advantages
- Shortest lookback period (2 years)
- No state income tax on trust income
- Favorable trust laws
- Privacy protections
- Professional trustee availability

## Dynasty Trusts
**NRS 111.1031**

### Rule Against Perpetuities
- **365-year rule against perpetuities**
- Essentially allows perpetual trusts
- Multi-generational wealth transfer

### Features
- Avoids estate tax at each generation
- GST tax exemption preserved
- Family governance provisions
- Asset protection for descendants
- Investment flexibility

### Planning Uses
- Multi-generational wealth transfer
- GST tax planning
- Asset protection for family
- Family business succession

## Community Property Rules
**NRS Chapter 123**

### Classification
- All property acquired during marriage is community property
- Separate property: before marriage, gifts, inheritance
- Commingling may convert character
- Written agreements can alter classification

### Income from Separate Property
- Income from separate property is **community property** (civil law rule)
- Same as Idaho, Louisiana, Texas

### Step-Up in Basis
- **Full step-up in basis** for community property at first spouse's death
- Both halves get stepped-up basis
- Significant income tax advantage

### Community Property Trust
- Available for married couples
- Clarifies property character
- Ensures full step-up benefit

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: NRS Chapter 162A (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (NRS 162A.220)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (NRS 162A.210)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (NRS 162A.620)
- Nevada provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (NRS 162A.400)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: NRS Chapter 162A and 449

### Types Available
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care Decisions (NRS 162A.700)
- Living Will (Declaration) (NRS 449.600)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements
**Healthcare POA (NRS 162A.790)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **notarized**
- Witnesses not required

**Living Will (NRS 449.610)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Nevada POLST
- Available in Nevada
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (NRS 449.626)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- **No state income tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- No income tax on trust income (including DAPTs)
- Popular situs for trusts from other states
- Dynasty trust planning attractive
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates

## Medicaid Planning (Nevada Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Nevada-Specific Programs
- **Nevada Medicaid** administered by DHCFP
- **Home and Community Based Waivers**
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Community Property Considerations
- Community property rules affect Medicaid planning
- Community spouse entitled to half
- Special rules for married couples

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $8,000-9,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**NRS 115.010**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$605,000** in equity protected
- Very strong protection compared to most states
- Applies to dwelling and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Declaration recommended but protection automatic
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

## Transfer on Death Deed
**NRS 111.655 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must designate beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for Nevada real property

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### General Probate (NRS Chapter 143)
- Court supervised administration
- Personal representative appointed by court
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Summary Administration (NRS 145.040)
- For estates under **$100,000** total value
- Simplified procedures
- Less court involvement

### Affidavit Procedure (NRS 146.080)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$25,000** in personal property
- 40-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Summary administration: 3-6 months
- Small estate affidavit: 2-3 months
- Creditor claim period: 90 days from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **District Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Asset Protection Trust State
- Top-tier DAPT jurisdiction
- 2-year lookback (shortest)
- No state income tax on trust income
- Professional trust industry

### Dynasty Trust State
- 365-year RAP essentially allows perpetual trusts
- GST tax planning
- Multi-generational wealth transfer

### No State Taxes
- No income, estate, inheritance, or gift tax
- Popular for establishing domicile
- Attractive for retirees and wealthy individuals

### Community Property with Step-Up
- Full basis step-up advantage

### Planning Considerations
- Consider Nevada DAPT for asset protection

- No state taxes makes Nevada attractive for domicile

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from district court required
- Nevada allows examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate
- Community property rules may apply to contents

### Community Property Note
- Nevada is community property state
- Box contents characterized as community or separate property
- Important for estate planning

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document community vs. separate property in box
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Nevada privacy laws affect disclosure requirements
