# North Dakota

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **40% Endowment Tax Credit** (one of highest in nation)
- **40% Planned Gift Tax Credit**
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Strong homestead protection ($150,000)
- Higher Medicaid asset limit ($3,000)
- Holographic wills recognized
- Uniform Probate Code adopted
- Agricultural estate planning significant

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: North Dakota Century Code Chapter 30.1 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (NDCC § 30.1-08-02)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (NDCC § 30.1-08-04)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (NDCC § 30.1-08-02)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required
- Practical for agricultural community

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness does not invalidate will
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: NDCC Chapter 59-09 et seq. (North Dakota Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (NDCC § 59-12-02)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- North Dakota trust law governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (NDCC § 59-13-02)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- North Dakota allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting
- Available under North Dakota law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: NDCC Chapter 30.1-30 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (NDCC § 30.1-30-05)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (NDCC § 30.1-30-04)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (NDCC § 30.1-30-61)
- North Dakota provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (NDCC § 30.1-30-20)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (NDCC § 30.1-30-40)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: NDCC Chapter 23-06.5 (Uniform Health-Care Decisions Act)

### Types Available
- Health Care Directive (combined form)
- Individual Instruction (Living Will)
- Power of Attorney for Health Care

### Execution Requirements (NDCC § 23-06.5-05)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Person who signed on behalf of principal
  - Healthcare provider
  - Related to principal

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### North Dakota POST
- Available in North Dakota
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (NDCC § 23-06.5-07)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Grandparent
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates
- Excellent charitable incentives (40% credits)
- Probate avoidance for privacy and efficiency

## Medicaid Planning (ND Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$3,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### North Dakota-Specific Programs
- **ND Medicaid** administered by DHS
- **SPED Waiver** (Service Payments for Elderly and Disabled)
- Home and Community Based Services
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $8,000-9,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**NDCC § 47-18-01 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$150,000** in value protected
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Strong protection

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Declaration not required but recommended
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Agricultural Protection
- Protects farm/ranch home
- Important for agricultural families

## Transfer on Death Deed
**NDCC § 47-10-21 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Practical for agricultural property

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must designate beneficiary

### Advantages
- Avoids probate for real property
- Simple and cost-effective
- Step-up in basis at death
- Revocable during lifetime

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for North Dakota real property

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (NDCC § 30.1-14-01)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by registrar
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (NDCC § 30.1-15-01)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (NDCC § 30.1-23-01)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Administration
- For small estates
- Simplified court process
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 3 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **District Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### 40% Endowment Tax Credit
- **Among highest charitable credit rates in nation**
- For contributions to qualified North Dakota endowments
- Maximum credit: $10,000 individual / $20,000 married filing jointly
- Applies to permanent endowment funds

### 40% Planned Gift Tax Credit
- Additional 40% credit for planned gifts
- Life income gifts (CRTs, gift annuities)
- Bequests
- Can be claimed in addition to endowment credit in some cases
- Exceptional incentive for estate planning gifts

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $3,000 individual limit more generous
- Provides additional planning flexibility

### Agricultural Focus
- Farm and ranch succession planning critical
- Holographic wills practical for rural areas
- TOD deeds useful for land transfers
- Entity structures (LLC, family partnerships) common

### Planning Considerations
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- 40% charitable credits exceptionally valuable
- TOD deeds widely used for real property
- Strong homestead provides protection
- Agricultural succession requires special attention
- Higher Medicaid asset limit advantageous

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Court appointment of personal representative required for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze on death
- Contents accessible without court involvement
- Important for farm/ranch succession planning

### Best Practices
- Avoid storing original will in safe deposit box
- Keep agricultural documents accessible to successors
- Document box contents
- Consider joint rental for business continuity

## State Charitable Incentives

### Endowment Tax Credit
- **40% credit** for contributions to qualified North Dakota endowments
- Maximum credit: $10,000 individual / $20,000 married filing jointly
- **One of highest charitable credit rates in the nation**
- Applies to permanent endowment funds

### Planned Gift Tax Credit
- Additional **40% credit** for planned gifts (life income gifts, bequests)
- Same maximum limits as endowment credit
- Can be claimed in addition to endowment credit in some cases
- Specifically rewards estate planning gifts

### Agricultural Research Contribution Credit
- Credit for donations supporting agricultural research
- Supports ND agricultural economy

### Estate Planning Implications
- 40% credit rate is among the highest nationally
- Planned gift credit specifically rewards estate planning gifts
- Excellent state for establishing endowments during lifetime
- No state death taxes + high charitable credits = very favorable environment
- Consider split-interest gifts (CRTs, CLTs) for maximum credit benefit
- Agricultural families can support land-grant university research
