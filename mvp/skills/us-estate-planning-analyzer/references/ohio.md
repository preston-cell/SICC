# Ohio

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax (repealed 2013)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **Transfer on Death Designation Affidavit** available
- TOD Affidavit for vehicles
- No holographic wills
- Probate court in each county

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Ohio Revised Code Chapter 2107

### Execution Requirements (ORC § 2107.03)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 competent witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (ORC § 2107.24)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Ohio
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be valid if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: ORC Chapter 5801 et seq. (Ohio Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (ORC § 5804.02)
- Ohio Trust Code adopted (based on UTC)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Ohio Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (ORC § 5806.02)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (ORC § 5808.08)
- Ohio allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (ORC § 5808.18)
- Authorized under Ohio Trust Code
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

### Legacy Trusts (Asset Protection) (ORC § 5816.01 et seq.)
- Ohio Legacy Trust Act
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts allowed
- Asset protection features
- 18-month lookback period

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: ORC Chapter 1337 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (ORC § 1337.25)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (ORC § 1337.24)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (ORC § 1337.60)
- Ohio provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (ORC § 1337.40)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (ORC § 1337.51)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: ORC Chapter 1337 and 2133

### Types Available
- Healthcare Power of Attorney (ORC § 1337.11)
- Living Will Declaration (ORC § 2133.02)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements
**Healthcare POA (ORC § 1337.12)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** AND **notarized**

**Living Will (ORC § 2133.02)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** AND **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be attending physician

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Ohio DNR
- Do-Not-Resuscitate available
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (ORC § 2133.08)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child (majority decides)
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling (majority decides)
  - Nearest adult relative

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (repealed January 1, 2013)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates
- Probate avoidance for privacy and efficiency
- Transfer on Death tools widely used

## Medicaid Planning (Ohio Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Ohio-Specific Programs
- **Ohio Medicaid** administered by ODM
- **PASSPORT** waiver for home-based services
- **MyCare Ohio** managed care
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $8,000-9,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**ORC § 2329.66**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$145,425** in equity protected (indexed for inflation)
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Increased periodically

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

## Transfer on Death Designation Affidavit
**ORC § 5302.22**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Called "Transfer on Death Designation Affidavit"

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must designate beneficiary

### Advantages
- Avoids probate for real property
- Simple and cost-effective
- Step-up in basis at death
- Revocable during lifetime

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for Ohio real property

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent affidavit supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Transfer on Death for Vehicles
**ORC § 2131.13**

### Features
- Transfer vehicles on death without probate
- Designate beneficiary on title
- Simple and effective

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Administration
- Available for many estates
- Less court supervision
- Personal representative has authority
- Most common form

### Formal Administration (ORC § 2113)
- Full court supervision
- Required for contested estates
- Bond typically required

### Summary Release from Administration (ORC § 2113.031)
- For estates under **$35,000** in personal property
- Surviving spouse as sole beneficiary
- Simplified procedure

### Small Estate Affidavit (ORC § 2113.03)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$35,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- Court filing required
- Release from administration

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months from appointment

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Court** in each county handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Transfer on Death Tools
- TOD Designation Affidavit for real property
- TOD for vehicles
- TOD for securities
- Widely used for probate avoidance

### Legacy Trusts
- Ohio Legacy Trust Act for asset protection
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts
- 18-month lookback (shorter than some states)

### Probate Court System
- Dedicated probate court in each county
- Specialized judges
- Generally efficient process

### Planning Considerations
- Transfer on Death tools widely used
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Legacy trusts for asset protection
- Moderate homestead exemption (indexed)
- Industrial and agricultural economy affects planning

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Ohio law allows limited access with death certificate to search for will
- Probate court in county of residence handles administration

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate court involvement
- Common approach for married couples

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Ohio probate courts recommend filing original will with court
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor of box location

## State Charitable Incentives

### Ohio Scholarship Donation Credit
- Credit for donations to scholarship granting organizations
- Supports private school scholarships
- Limited credits available statewide

### Historic Preservation Tax Credit
- Credit for rehabilitation of historic buildings
- Can apply to donated preservation easements
- Up to 25% of qualified expenditures

### Small Business Investment Credit
- Credit for investments in small businesses
- Supports Ohio economic development

### Estate Planning Implications
- Limited state charitable credits currently available
- Focus on federal charitable planning strategies
- Transfer on Death Deeds can coordinate with charitable planning
- No state death taxes means federal planning is primary focus
- Historic preservation credits valuable for owners of qualifying properties
