# Indiana

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax (repealed 2022)
- Common law property state
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Simple probate procedures
- Modest homestead exemption ($22,750)
- Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Strong retirement account protections

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Indiana Code Title 29 (Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (IC 29-1-5-3)
- Testator must be **18+ years old** (or younger if member of armed forces/merchant marine)
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be **attested and subscribed** by **2 or more competent witnesses** in presence of testator

### Self-Proving Affidavit (IC 29-1-5-3.1)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate
- Must be executed at same time as will or later

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Indiana
- All wills must be witnessed
- Foreign holographic wills may be recognized if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness allowed but may affect gift to witness
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Must sign in presence of testator

### Foreign Wills (IC 29-1-5-1.5)
- Valid if executed in compliance with:
  - Indiana law, OR
  - Law of state/country where executed, OR
  - Law of testator's domicile at execution or death

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Indiana Code Title 30, Article 4 (Indiana Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (IC 30-4-2-1)
- Indiana Trust Code adopted (based on UTC)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required (oral trusts limited)

### Trust Administration
- Indiana Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (IC 30-4-2-13)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (IC 30-4-2.5)
- Indiana allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor/protector can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Useful for investment management

### Trust Decanting
- Authorized under Indiana law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Requires trustee discretion
- Various limitations apply

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: IC 30-5-1 et seq.

### Execution Requirements (IC 30-5-4-1)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** or witnessed by **2 adults**
- Notarization recommended for real estate transactions
- Must clearly identify agent

### Durability (IC 30-5-4-2)
- Durable if includes language: "This power of attorney shall not be affected by subsequent disability or incapacity of the principal"
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form
- Indiana provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties should accept valid POA

### Agent Duties (IC 30-5-8)
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Create or change survivorship rights

### Third Party Acceptance (IC 30-5-9)
- Third parties must accept valid POA in good faith
- May request certification
- Limited grounds for refusal

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: IC 16-36-4 (Health Care Consent); IC 16-36-7 (Living Wills)

### Types Available
- Living Will Declaration - IC 16-36-4
- Healthcare Representative Appointment
- Combined Advance Directive

### Living Will Requirements (IC 16-36-4-10)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses must not be:
  - Related by blood or marriage
  - Entitled to any portion of estate
  - Responsible for healthcare costs
  - Attending physician or employee

### Healthcare Representative (IC 16-36-1-7)
- Statutory form available
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Agent cannot be healthcare provider unless related

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### POST (Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment)
- Available in Indiana
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Replaces previous out-of-hospital DNR orders

### Default Surrogate (IC 16-36-1-5)
- If no healthcare representative designated:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Religious superior (for members of religious orders)
  - Nearest living relative

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax** (repealed January 1, 2022)
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Historical Note
- Indiana inheritance tax was phased out 2013-2021
- Fully repealed as of January 1, 2022
- Deaths before 2022 may have been subject to inheritance tax

## Medicaid Planning (Indiana Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Indiana-Specific Programs
- **Aged and Disabled Waiver**
- **CHOICE (Community and Home Options to Institutional Care for the Elderly and Disabled)**
- **Traumatic Brain Injury Waiver**
- Estate recovery program active
- PACE programs in some areas

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $8,000-9,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**IC 34-55-10-2**

### Exemption Amount
- **$22,750** equity protected
- Applies to real estate used as residence
- Includes mobile homes

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must own or be purchasing the property
- Only one homestead per family
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony
  - Judgments for purchase price

### Tenancy by Entirety
- Recognized in Indiana
- Property held by married couples
- Protected from individual creditors of one spouse
- Additional creditor protection beyond homestead

## Transfer on Death Deed
**IC 32-17-14**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must designate beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Property must be located in Indiana

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording revocation
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Sale of property revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Unsupervised Administration (IC 29-1-7.5-2)
- Default for most estates
- Minimal court supervision
- Personal representative acts independently
- Must provide notice to heirs
- Most estates use unsupervised administration

### Supervised Administration
- Required if unsupervised not appropriate
- Full court supervision
- Court approval for distributions
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (IC 29-1-8-1)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$100,000**
- 45-day waiting period after death
- No real property (or real property disposed by other means)
- Court filing not required
- Simplified transfer procedure

### Summary Administration
- For estates that can pay all claims
- Simplified procedures
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Unsupervised administration: 6-12 months typical
- Supervised administration: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 3 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Superior Court** or **Circuit Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### No Death Taxes
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax (repealed 2022)
- One of states with no death tax at state level
- Only federal estate tax applies

### Tenancy by Entirety
- Strong creditor protection for married couples
- Property held as TBE protected from individual creditors
- Both spouses must consent to transfer
- Consider for asset protection planning

### Strong Retirement Protections
- Qualified retirement accounts protected
- IRAs have substantial protection
- Protections apply during life and at death

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Available for all real property
- Simple probate avoidance tool
- Can be combined with or instead of trust

### Planning Considerations
- No state death tax means less complex planning
- TOD deeds effective for real property
- Unsupervised administration reduces probate costs
- Tenancy by entirety for married couple asset protection
- Consider revocable trust for privacy and multi-property estates
- Strong retirement account protections

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death (IC 32-17-10)
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate
- Can search for will, burial instructions
- Inventory required before removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate
- Consider liability exposure

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor/personal representative of location
- Consider adding fiduciary as deputy

## State Charitable Incentives

### Neighborhood Assistance Credit (NAC)
- 50% credit for donations to approved neighborhood organizations
- Maximum credit varies by project type
- Supports community development in designated areas
- Credit must be pre-approved

### College Choice 529 Education Savings Credit
- 20% credit on contributions to Indiana 529 plans
- Maximum $1,500 credit per year ($7,500 contribution)
- While not charitable, supports education giving

### Estate Planning Implications
- NAC provides significant credit for qualified donations
- Pre-approval requirement means advance planning necessary
- Coordinate lifetime charitable giving with Indiana residency
- No state estate tax means federal planning is primary focus
