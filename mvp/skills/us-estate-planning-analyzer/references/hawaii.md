# Hawaii

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has state estate tax** (exemption ~$5.49M - LOWER than federal)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **Highest top estate tax rate in nation**: 20%
- **Portability available** for state estate tax (unique among states)
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Holographic wills recognized
- Hawaiian Home Lands considerations

## Estate Tax
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 236E

### Exemption (2025)
- **$5,490,000** (tied to 2017 federal amount, frozen)
- **Much lower than federal** $13.99M exemption
- Creates significant disparities for Hawaii estates

### Tax Rates (Progressive - Highest in Nation)
| Taxable Amount | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $1M | 10% |
| $1M - $2.5M | 13% |
| $2.5M - $5M | 14% |
| $5M - $7M | 15% |
| $7M - $10M | 16% |
| Over $10M | **20%** |

### Portability (Unique Among State Estate Taxes)
- Hawaii allows **Deceased Spousal Unused Exclusion (DSUE)**
- Surviving spouse can use deceased spouse's unused exemption
- **Must elect on Hawaii estate tax return (Form M-6)**
- Combined ~$11M exemption for married couples

### Filing Requirements
- File if taxable estate > $5,490,000
- File if federal Form 706 required and federal tax due
- **Due 9 months after death**

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 560 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (HRS § 560:2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator**
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time

### Self-Proving Affidavit (HRS § 560:2-504)
- Available and recommended for efficient probate
- Eliminates need for witness testimony

### Holographic Wills (HRS § 560:2-503)
- **Recognized** if signature and material portions in testator's handwriting
- No witness requirement

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 554D (Hawaii Uniform Trust Code - effective Jan 1, 2022)

### Features
- Based on 2018 Uniform Trust Code
- Reduces complexity and cost of trust proceedings
- Allows nonjudicial resolution of trust issues

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 551E (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (HRS § 551E-3)
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Notarization **not legally required** but strongly recommended
- Many financial institutions require notarization

### Durability
- All POAs under Chapter 551E are **durable by default**
- Only terminates if expressly states it will upon incapacity

### Important Note
- Statutory POA does **NOT** authorize healthcare decisions
- Requires separate healthcare directive

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 327E (Uniform Health-Care Decisions Act)

### Execution Requirements (HRS § 327E-3)
- Must be **signed and dated**
- **Notarized** OR witnessed by **2 adult witnesses**

### Witness Restrictions
- Cannot be healthcare agent
- Cannot be healthcare provider or employee
- One witness cannot be relative or have inheritance rights

### Agent Restrictions
- Unless related, agent cannot be owner/operator/employee of healthcare institution where principal receives care

## Medicaid Planning (Med-QUEST)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married: CSRA up to federal maximum

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings
- Prepaid burial

### Hawaii-Specific Considerations
- **Expanded estate recovery state** - can recover from non-probate assets
- High cost of living significantly affects planning
- Very high nursing home costs
- Island-specific considerations for care options
- Limited care options on smaller islands
- May need to relocate for appropriate care

## Homestead Protection

### Homestead Allowance (HRS § 560:2-402)
- Surviving spouse: **$15,000**
- Limited compared to some states

### Exempt Property (HRS § 560:2-403)
- Surviving spouse entitled to **$20,000** in household items
- Priority over all claims against estate

## Probate Procedures

### Small Estate Affidavit (HRS § 560:3-1201)
- Available for estates **under $100,000** in gross value
- Motor vehicles excluded from threshold
- 30-day waiting period
- No court proceedings required

### Informal Probate (HRS § 560:3-301)
- Unsupervised administration for uncontested estates
- Personal representative has broader authority

## Transfer on Death Deed
**Statutory Basis**: HRS Chapter 527

### Features
- Available for real property
- Revocable during lifetime
- Nontestamentary (no court involvement)
- Avoids probate
- Must be recorded

## Hawaiian Home Lands Considerations

### Overview
- Established by Congress in 1921
- Managed by Department of Hawaiian Home Lands (DHHL)
- Available to Native Hawaiians (at least 50% Hawaiian blood)
- 99-year lease terms at $1/year

### Estate Planning Implications
- Property cannot be conveyed like regular real estate
- Complex successorship issues
- Must work with DHHL
- Long waiting periods for applications
- **No property taxes for first 7 years** after lease award

## Unique State Features

### High Estate Tax Rates
- 20% top rate is highest in nation
- Significant planning needed for large estates
- Portability helps married couples

### Portability
- **Unique among states** with estate tax
- Must file Hawaii estate tax return to elect
- Can significantly reduce tax burden for couples

### High Cost of Living
- Affects Medicaid planning significantly
- Higher nursing home costs
- Higher penalty divisor

### Island Considerations
- Care options may be limited on smaller islands
- Transportation costs for family
- May require inter-island relocation for care

### Planning Considerations
- **Portability election essential** for married couples
- Credit shelter trusts still useful despite portability
- High rates justify aggressive tax planning
- **ILIT for life insurance critical**
- Consider out-of-state trust situs for income tax
- Lifetime gifting to reduce estate below exemption
