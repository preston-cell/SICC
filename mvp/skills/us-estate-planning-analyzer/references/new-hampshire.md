# New Hampshire

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- **No state income tax** (interest/dividends tax phasing out by 2027)
- **No state sales tax**
- Common law property state
- Tax-friendly state for retirees
- Limited homestead protection ($120,000)
- No holographic wills

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: New Hampshire Revised Statutes Annotated Title 56, Chapter 551

### Execution Requirements (RSA 551:2)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 credible witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (RSA 551:2-a)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in New Hampshire
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be valid if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be "credible" witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: RSA Chapter 564-B (New Hampshire Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (RSA 564-B:4-402)
- New Hampshire Trust Code adopted (based on UTC)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- New Hampshire Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (RSA 564-B:6-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (RSA 564-B:8-808)
- New Hampshire allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (RSA 564-B:4-418)
- Authorized under New Hampshire law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

### Qualified Dispositions Trust (RSA 564-D)
- New Hampshire DAPT available
- Self-settled spendthrift trust
- Asset protection features
- 4-year lookback period

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: RSA Chapter 564-E (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (RSA 564-E:105)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (RSA 564-E:104)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (RSA 564-E:301)
- New Hampshire provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (RSA 564-E:201)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (RSA 564-E:120)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: RSA Chapter 137-J (Advance Directives for Health Care Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Directive for Health Care (combined form)
- Living Will
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care

### Execution Requirements (RSA 137-J:14)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Spouse or heir
  - Healthcare provider
  - Person who would benefit financially

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### New Hampshire POLST
- Available in New Hampshire
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (RSA 137-J:32)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Grandparent
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- **No state income tax** (interest/dividends tax phasing out)
- **No state sales tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Income Tax Note
- Interest and Dividends Tax being phased out
- 3% rate in 2024, 2% in 2025, 1% in 2026
- Fully eliminated January 1, 2027
- Will have no broad-based income tax

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Very tax-friendly environment overall
- Popular retirement destination
- Border with MA creates domicile planning opportunities
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates

## Medicaid Planning (New Hampshire Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,500
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### New Hampshire-Specific Programs
- **NH Medicaid** administered by DHHS
- **Choices for Independence** waiver program
- Home and Community Based Services
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $11,000-12,000/month (verify current rate - higher cost state)

## Homestead Protection
**RSA 480:1 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$120,000** in equity protected
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Moderate protection compared to other states

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not specifically authorized by statute** in New Hampshire
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Full Administration (RSA Chapter 553)
- Court supervised administration
- Personal representative (executor/administrator) appointed
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required unless waived

### Summary Administration (RSA 553:32)
- For estates under **$10,000** in personal property
- Surviving spouse or heirs can request
- Simplified procedures
- Less court involvement

### Voluntary Administration (RSA 553:31)
- For small estates
- Affidavit procedure available
- Court filing may not be required

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Summary administration: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months from appointment

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Division of Circuit Court** handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Tax-Friendly State
- No income tax (interest/dividends phasing out)
- No sales tax
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- Very attractive for domicile planning

### Border State Opportunities
- Border with Massachusetts creates planning opportunities
- MA has $2M estate tax with cliff effect
- NH residents can avoid MA death taxes
- Consider NH domicile for wealthy MA residents

### Popular Retirement Destination
- Low tax burden attracts retirees
- Domicile change from high-tax states
- Quality of life considerations

### Planning Considerations
- Tax-friendly environment for estate planning
- Border state opportunities with MA
- No TOD deed - use trusts for real property
- DAPT available for asset protection
- Domicile planning attractive

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Administrator/executor needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- No state estate or inheritance tax simplifies contents planning
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor of box location
