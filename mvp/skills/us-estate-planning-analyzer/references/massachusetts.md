# Massachusetts

## Key Differences from Other States
- **State estate tax** with $2M exemption (one of lowest in nation)
- **CLIFF EFFECT** - entire estate taxed once threshold exceeded
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- No portability for state estate tax
- Declared homestead provides significant protection ($500K)

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: MGL Chapter 190B (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (MGL c. 190B, § 2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (MGL c. 190B, § 2-504)
- Available and strongly recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Massachusetts
- All wills must be witnessed
- Foreign holographic wills may be challenged

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not invalidate will but may create presumption of undue influence

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: MGL Chapter 203E (Massachusetts Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (MGL c. 203E, § 402)
- Massachusetts Uniform Trust Code adopted (2012)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Massachusetts UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon death

### Revocable Trusts (MGL c. 203E, § 602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- Massachusetts allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (MGL c. 203E, § 816A)
- Authorized under Massachusetts UTC
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: MGL c. 190B, Article V

### Execution Requirements (MGL c. 190B, § 5-501)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 disinterested witnesses**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- All three required

### Durability (MGL c. 190B, § 5-501)
- Must include specific durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form
- Massachusetts provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties may resist non-statutory forms

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Delegate authority

## Healthcare Proxy
**Statutory Basis**: MGL Chapter 201D

### Execution Requirements (MGL c. 201D, § 2)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Healthcare agent
  - Healthcare provider

### Healthcare Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Massachusetts MOLST
- Medical Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment
- Available for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Pink form

### Default Surrogate (MGL c. 201D, § 6)
- If no healthcare proxy:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Other relative or close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Massachusetts Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $2,000,000 (one of lowest - tied with Oregon)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%

### CLIFF EFFECT - CRITICAL
- **Once estate exceeds $2M, ENTIRE estate is taxed from $0**
- Not just the excess over $2M
- Creates dramatic tax consequence at threshold

**Example:**
| Estate Value | MA Estate Tax |
|--------------|---------------|
| $1,999,999 | $0 |
| $2,000,001 | ~$99,600 |
| $2,500,000 | ~$125,800 |
| $3,000,000 | ~$182,000 |

### Credit Against Tax
- $99,600 credit available (effective 2023)
- Reduces but does not eliminate cliff effect
- Effective exemption approximately $2M

### Rate Structure
- Graduated rates from 0.8% to 16%
- Based on federal credit for state death taxes table
- Rates increase with estate size

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Massachusetts Department of Revenue
- Must file if gross estate exceeds $2M

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- **Cliff effect creates urgency** for estates near $2M
- Low threshold affects many middle-class families
- Greater Boston real estate values push many over threshold
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- Consider domicile change for very large estates

## Medicaid Planning (MassHealth)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### MassHealth-Specific Programs
- **MassHealth** is Massachusetts Medicaid program
- **PACE** programs available
- **Home and Community Based Waivers**
- Estate recovery program active (MassHealth Estate Recovery Unit)

### Irrevocable Trust Requirements
- Must be truly irrevocable
- Grantor cannot access principal
- 5+ years before MassHealth application

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $13,000-14,000/month (verify current rate - high cost state)

## Homestead Protection
**MGL Chapter 188**

### Automatic Homestead (MGL c. 188, § 4)
- **$125,000** protection automatic
- Applies without filing
- Limited but immediate protection

### Declared Homestead (MGL c. 188, § 3)
- Up to **$500,000** protection
- **Must be recorded** at Registry of Deeds
- Requires filing declaration
- Significant planning opportunity

### Elderly and Disabled (MGL c. 188, § 1)
- Same $500,000 limit
- Automatic for those 62+ if declared
- Declaration recommended for maximum protection

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner (or have ownership interest)
- Only one homestead per family
- Must record declaration for full protection

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Federal tax liens
  - Debts secured before homestead

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (MGL c. 190B, § 3-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by register
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (MGL c. 190B, § 3-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (MGL c. 190B, § 3-1201)
- **Voluntary administration** for estates under **$25,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Simplified procedure

### Summary Administration
- For small estates
- Simplified procedures
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 1 year from death

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate and Family Court** handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Planning Strategies

### Avoiding the Cliff Effect
- Lifetime gifting to reduce estate below $2M
- Irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT)
- Charitable giving
- Credit shelter trust for married couples
- Qualified personal residence trust (QPRT)

### Credit Shelter Trust
- Essential for married couples
- Preserves each spouse's $2M exemption
- No portability makes this critical
- Fund at first spouse's death

### Tax Calculation Example
Estate: $3,000,000
- MA estate tax: ~$182,000
- Effective rate: ~6%

With proper planning (gifts reduce to $1.9M):
- MA estate tax: $0
- Savings: $182,000

## Unique State Features

### Cliff Effect Creates Urgency
- $2M threshold affects many middle-class families
- Real estate values in Greater Boston area significant
- Home equity alone may push over threshold
- Planning essential for estates near $2M

### Low Threshold
- One of lowest exemptions in nation (tied with Oregon)
- Many states have no estate tax
- Consider domicile planning for wealthy clients

### Declared Homestead
- $500,000 protection requires filing
- Significant creditor protection available
- Must be proactive

### Planning Considerations
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- ILIT to remove life insurance from estate
- Consider lifetime gifting for estates near threshold
- Declared homestead for creditor protection
- Real estate values often trigger estate tax
- No portability means both spouses must plan

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Massachusetts allows limited access with death certificate to search for will
- Personal representative appointment needed for removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate

### Estate Tax Considerations
- Massachusetts has $2M estate tax threshold with cliff effect
- Contents valued for estate tax - critical given low threshold
- Document all valuable items carefully

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Carefully document contents for estate tax purposes
- Low $2M threshold makes valuation critical
- Inform personal representative of box location
