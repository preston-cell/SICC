# Pennsylvania

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- **Has state inheritance tax** (rates 0-15% by class)
- Common law property state
- Spouse completely exempt (0%)
- No exemption for non-spouses (taxed from first dollar)
- **No homestead exemption**
- No holographic wills
- Register of Wills handles probate

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: 20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 25 (Wills)

### Execution Requirements (20 Pa.C.S. § 2502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by mark) at the end
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Testator must sign or acknowledge in presence of witnesses

### Self-Proving Affidavit
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Pennsylvania
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be challenged

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence (or after acknowledgment)
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: 20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 77 (Pennsylvania Uniform Trust Act)

### Trust Creation (20 Pa.C.S. § 7732)
- Pennsylvania Uniform Trust Act adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- PA Uniform Trust Act governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (20 Pa.C.S. § 7752)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (20 Pa.C.S. § 7778)
- Pennsylvania allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (20 Pa.C.S. § 7780.3)
- Authorized under Pennsylvania law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: 20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 56 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (20 Pa.C.S. § 5601)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- All three required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (20 Pa.C.S. § 5604)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (20 Pa.C.S. § 5610)
- Pennsylvania provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (20 Pa.C.S. § 5603)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Delegate authority

### Third Party Acceptance (20 Pa.C.S. § 5608)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: 20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 54 (Health Care)

### Types Available
- Healthcare Power of Attorney (20 Pa.C.S. § 5456)
- Living Will (Declaration) (20 Pa.C.S. § 5442)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements
**Healthcare POA (20 Pa.C.S. § 5456)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Notarization not required but recommended

**Living Will (20 Pa.C.S. § 5442)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Pennsylvania POLST
- Available in Pennsylvania (POST form)
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (20 Pa.C.S. § 5461)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Adult grandchild
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### No Estate Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- Pennsylvania repealed estate tax

### Pennsylvania Inheritance Tax
- **State inheritance tax applies**
- Flat rates based on relationship to decedent
- **No exemption amounts** for non-spouse (tax from first dollar)
- Collected by PA Department of Revenue

### Tax Rates by Beneficiary Class
| Beneficiary | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Surviving spouse | **0%** (fully exempt) |
| Children, grandchildren, parents | **4.5%** |
| Siblings | **12%** |
| All others (nieces, nephews, friends, etc.) | **15%** |

### Key Inheritance Tax Points
- **Spouse pays nothing** (fully exempt)
- Lineal descendants (children, grandchildren) pay 4.5%
- Parents pay 4.5%
- Siblings pay 12%
- All others (nieces, nephews, friends, partners) pay 15%
- **No threshold** - tax applies from first dollar (except spouse)

### 5% Early Payment Discount
- **5% discount** if inheritance tax paid within **3 months** of death
- Significant savings opportunity
- Plan estate liquidity accordingly

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- 5% discount for payment within 3 months
- File with PA Department of Revenue
- Due with REV-1500 form

### Planning Implications
- **Maximize bequests to spouse** (0% rate)
- Lineal descendants preferable to siblings or others
- Consider trusts for higher-rate beneficiaries
- Life insurance owned by beneficiary may avoid tax
- Take advantage of 5% early payment discount
- Charitable bequests avoid inheritance tax

## Medicaid Planning (PA Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$2,400** (slightly higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Pennsylvania Gift Exception
- PA allows **$500/month gifting** without penalty
- Unique exception to federal rules
- Valuable for Medicaid planning

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Pennsylvania-Specific Programs
- **PA Medicaid** (Medical Assistance) administered by DHS
- **LIFE** (Living Independence for the Elderly)
- **OBRA Waiver** for home-based services
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $10,000-11,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
- **No general homestead exemption** in Pennsylvania
- One of few states without homestead protection
- Very limited creditor protection for residence
- Tenancy by entirety provides some protection for married couples

### Alternative Protections
- **Tenancy by entirety** for married couples (significant)
- Trust ownership
- Retirement account protections (unlimited for qualified plans)
- Life insurance protections

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not specifically authorized** in Pennsylvania
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Probate Administration (20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 33)
- **Register of Wills** in each county handles probate
- Executor/administrator appointed by Register
- Inventory required
- Accounting required

### Short Certificates
- Register issues short certificates (letters)
- Used to access accounts, transfer property

### Small Estate Procedures (20 Pa.C.S. § 3102)
- **Petition procedure** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- Simplified procedures
- No real property included

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 1 year from advertisement

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Register of Wills** handles probate in each county
- **Orphans' Court** for contested matters
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### No Homestead Protection
- Major planning consideration
- One of few states without protection
- Tenancy by entirety is primary protection for married couples
- Trust planning more important

### 5% Early Payment Discount
- Significant benefit for early inheritance tax payment
- Plan estate liquidity to take advantage
- 3-month deadline

### $500/Month Gift Exception
- Unique Medicaid rule
- Allows small monthly gifts without penalty
- Valuable planning tool

### Spousal Exemption Complete
- Spouse pays 0% inheritance tax
- Maximize spousal bequests when possible

### Planning Considerations
- Maximize bequests to spouse (0% rate)
- Structure gifts to lower-rate beneficiaries
- Consider trusts for 15% rate beneficiaries
- Take advantage of 5% early payment discount
- PA gift exception for Medicaid planning
- No homestead - use tenancy by entirety, trusts

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from Register of Wills required
- Pennsylvania allows examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without Register of Wills involvement

### Inheritance Tax Note
- Pennsylvania has inheritance tax - document contents for tax reporting
- Spouse exempt (0%), children/parents 4.5%, siblings 12%, others 15%
- 5% discount if inheritance tax paid within 3 months

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document valuable contents for inheritance tax purposes
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider early payment discount for inheritance tax
