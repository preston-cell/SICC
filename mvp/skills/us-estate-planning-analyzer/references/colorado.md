# Colorado

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state (but allows marital agreements)
- Strong homestead protection with higher limits for elderly
- Beneficiary deed available
- Informal probate process available
- Designated beneficiary agreements for unmarried couples
- Uniform Probate Code adopted

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Colorado Revised Statutes Title 15 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (CRS § 15-11-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another in testator's conscious presence at direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing signature or acknowledgment

### Self-Proving Affidavit (CRS § 15-11-504)
- Available and recommended for efficient probate
- Testator and witnesses execute affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (CRS § 15-11-502(2))
- **Recognized** if material portions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required
- Testamentary intent must be evident

### Electronic Wills
- Colorado enacted Uniform Electronic Wills Act (effective 2022)
- Electronic wills valid if properly executed
- Must be witnessed electronically
- Remote witnessing permitted

### Notarized Wills (CRS § 15-11-502(3))
- Will acknowledged by testator before notary is valid
- Alternative to traditional witnessing
- Notary signature satisfies witness requirement

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: CRS Title 15, Article 5 (Colorado Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (CRS § 15-5-402)
- Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries
- Must be in writing for real property (oral trusts for personal property possible)

### Trust Administration
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- 60-day notice requirement upon death

### Revocable Trusts (CRS § 15-5-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless expressly irrevocable
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Pet Trusts (CRS § 15-11-901)
- Valid and enforceable
- Terminates upon death of all designated animals
- Court may reduce amount if excessive

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: CRS § 15-14-701 et seq. (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (CRS § 15-14-705)
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **notarized**
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Durable if includes durability language

### Statutory Form (CRS § 15-14-741)
- Colorado provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA or face liability

### Durability (CRS § 15-14-704)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available (effective upon incapacity)

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (CRS § 15-14-720)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship
  - Disclaim property

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: CRS § 15-18.5-101 et seq. (Colorado Medical Treatment Decision Act)

### Types Available
- Medical Durable Power of Attorney
- Living Will/Declaration as to Medical Treatment
- Combined Advance Directive
- CPR Directive

### Execution Requirements (CRS § 15-14-506)
- Living will and healthcare proxy combined available
- Must be **signed and witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be agent
- Statutory form available

### Living Will Effectiveness
- Operative when:
  1. Terminal condition exists, AND
  2. Death would occur without life-sustaining procedures, AND
  3. Patient cannot make own decisions

### CPR Directive (CRS § 15-18.6-101)
- Specific directive regarding cardiopulmonary resuscitation
- For use in emergency situations
- Must be signed by declarant and physician
- Portable medical order

### Mental Health Declaration (CRS § 15-18.5-101)
- Separate from healthcare directive
- Addresses mental health treatment preferences
- Agent can make mental health decisions

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (linked to federal credit, effectively repealed)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

## Medicaid Planning (Health First Colorado)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Miller Trust (Qualified Income Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Colorado-Specific Programs
- **Health First Colorado** is Medicaid program name
- **Home and Community Based Services (HCBS)** waivers available
- **PACE** (Program of All-inclusive Care for Elderly) available in some areas
- Brain injury waiver program

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $9,000-10,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**CRS § 38-41-201 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Standard: Up to **$250,000** equity protected
- **Elderly (60+) and disabled**: Up to **$350,000** protected
- Adjusted periodically for inflation

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Automatic protection (no filing required)
- Only one homestead per family
- Must be occupied as residence

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - HOA liens (in some cases)
  - Child support/alimony

### Mobile Homes
- Mobile home on owned or leased land qualifies
- Same exemption amounts apply
- Must be principal residence

## Beneficiary Deed
**CRS § 15-15-401 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Must be **recorded before death**
- Must designate grantee-beneficiary by name or class

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Can be contested

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording instrument of revocation
- Subsequent beneficiary deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (CRS § 15-12-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by registrar
- Most common form of probate
- Supervised by court only on request

### Formal Probate (CRS § 15-12-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming
- Formal adjudication of will validity

### Small Estate Procedures (CRS § 15-12-1201)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$80,000** in personal property (2025)
- 10-day waiting period after death
- No real property can be included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Administration (CRS § 15-12-1202)
- For small estates with real property
- Simplified court process
- Faster than full administration

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 1-2 years or longer
- Small estate: 2-4 months

## Designated Beneficiary Agreements
**CRS § 15-22-101 et seq.**

### Purpose
- Legal protections for **unmarried couples**
- Not limited to same-sex couples
- Creates specific legal rights without marriage

### Rights Created
- Hospital visitation
- Medical decision-making authority
- Right to inherit (intestacy)
- Ability to sue for wrongful death
- Property rights

### Requirements
- Both parties must be 18+
- Cannot be married to each other or anyone else
- Must be in exclusive relationship
- Written agreement required

### Termination
- By written agreement of both parties
- By marriage to each other
- Upon death of either party
- Upon marriage of either party to another person

## Unique State Features

### Informal Probate
- Highly efficient process
- Minimal court involvement
- Lower costs than formal probate
- Fast resolution for uncontested estates

### TABOR (Taxpayer's Bill of Rights)
- Constitutional amendment limiting government spending
- Affects state services including some Medicaid programs
- May impact future estate planning laws

### Electronic Wills
- Progressive approach to estate planning
- Remote witnessing permitted
- Enables estate planning for remote residents

### Planning Considerations
- Beneficiary deeds useful for simple real property transfers
- Informal probate makes probate less burdensome than some states
- Consider designated beneficiary agreement for unmarried couples
- Higher homestead exemption for elderly provides planning opportunity
- HCBS waivers may be alternative to nursing home Medicaid

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary or court order required
- Bank may permit examination of contents with death certificate to locate will
- Cannot remove contents without proper authority

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- Contents not automatically frozen at one owner's death
- Joint renter can remove contents without court involvement
- Consider liability exposure when adding joint renters

### Best Practices
- Do NOT store original will in safe deposit box
- Document box contents and share with executor
- Consider naming fiduciary as authorized accessor
- Include safe deposit box in trust if applicable

## State Charitable Incentives

### Child Care Contribution Credit (CRS § 39-22-121)
- Credit for contributions to qualifying child care facilities
- Up to **50%** of contribution
- Maximum credit: **$100,000**
- Transferable and refundable for certain contributions

### Enterprise Zone Contribution Credit (CRS § 39-30-103.5)
- **25%** credit for cash contributions to enterprise zone administrators
- Supports economic development in designated areas
- Can be carried forward up to 5 years

### Colorado Charitable Gaming
- Certain charitable gaming proceeds support nonprofits
- Limited direct tax benefit but supports charitable sector

### Colorado Gives Day
- Annual statewide giving event
- Incentive funds boost donations
- December each year

### Estate Planning Implications
- Enterprise zone credits can significantly enhance charitable giving efficiency
- Coordinate lifetime giving with Colorado residency for maximum benefit
- Child care contributions may benefit family foundations focused on education
- Consider charitable remainder trusts for appreciated assets
