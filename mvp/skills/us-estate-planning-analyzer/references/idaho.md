# Idaho

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Community property state**
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property abolished (dower/curtesy)
- Strong homestead protections
- Simple probate procedures (Uniform Probate Code)
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Income from separate property is community property (Civil Law Rule)

## Community Property Rules

### Classification (Idaho Code § 32-906)
- All property acquired during marriage is community property
- Separate property includes: property owned before marriage, gifts, inheritance
- Income from separate property is **community property** (Civil Law Rule - unlike California)
- Commingling can convert separate to community property
- Transmutation requires clear intent

### Estate Planning Implications
- Each spouse owns 50% of community property
- **Full step-up in basis** for both halves at first spouse's death (IRC § 1014(b)(6))
- Each spouse can dispose of their 50% by will
- Cannot disinherit spouse's 50% share
- Income rule makes separate property planning more complex

### Quasi-Community Property
- Not specifically recognized in Idaho
- Property acquired elsewhere may be treated differently
- Consider characterization when moving to Idaho

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Idaho Code Title 15 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (Idaho Code § 15-2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old** (or emancipated minor)
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time of witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Idaho Code § 15-2-504)
- Available and recommended for probate efficiency
- Testator and witnesses sign before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (Idaho Code § 15-2-502(2))
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required
- Testamentary intent must be evident

### Electronic Wills
- Idaho has adopted provisions for electronic wills
- Must meet specific authentication requirements
- Remote witnessing may be permitted

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Idaho Code Title 15, Chapter 7 (Idaho Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Idaho Code § 15-7-401)
- Idaho Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Community Property Trusts
- Can hold community property and maintain character
- Important for preserving step-up in basis
- Both spouses should be co-trustees
- Critical to preserve CP character in trust document

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Idaho Code § 15-12-101 et seq. (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (Idaho Code § 15-12-105)
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Durable if includes durability language

### Statutory Form (Idaho Code § 15-12-301)
- Idaho provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Durability (Idaho Code § 15-12-104)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available (effective upon incapacity)

### Hot Powers (Idaho Code § 15-12-201)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship
  - Delegate authority

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Idaho Code § 39-4501 et seq. (Idaho Natural Death Act)

### Types Available
- Living Will (Declaration)
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements (Idaho Code § 39-4505)
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be related by blood or marriage
- One witness cannot be attending physician
- One witness cannot be employee of healthcare facility

### Living Will Effectiveness
- Operative when:
  1. Terminal condition exists, AND
  2. Death would occur without life-sustaining treatment, AND
  3. Patient cannot make own decisions

### Healthcare Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records (HIPAA authorization recommended)
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement

### POLST (Provider Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment)
- Available in Idaho
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

## Medicaid Planning (Idaho Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Idaho-Specific Programs
- **Idaho Medicaid** administered by Department of Health and Welfare
- **Aged and Disabled Waiver** for home and community-based services
- Estate recovery program active
- Lower cost of living affects penalty calculations

### Community Property and Medicaid
- All community property considered available to institutionalized spouse
- CSRA protects community spouse's share
- Proper characterization critical
- May need to convert CP to separate property for protection

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $7,500-8,500/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Idaho Code § 55-1001 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$175,000** equity in dwelling
- **Elderly (65+) and disabled**: Up to **$250,000** protected
- Applies to dwelling house and land

### Requirements
- Must be **owner** (or head of family)
- Must be **primary residence**
- Automatic protection (no filing required, but declaration recommended)
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony
  - Debts incurred before homestead declared

### Declaration
- Recording declaration recommended but not required
- Provides clearer protection
- Must describe property and state it's homestead

## Transfer on Death Deed
**Idaho Code § 55-2701 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Must be **recorded before death**
- Must designate beneficiary by name or class

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Can be contested

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording instrument of revocation
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (Idaho Code § 15-3-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by registrar
- Most common form of probate
- Supervised by court only on request

### Formal Probate (Idaho Code § 15-3-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming
- Formal adjudication of will validity

### Small Estate Procedures (Idaho Code § 15-3-1201)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$100,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Administration
- For small estates with real property
- Simplified court process
- Faster than full administration

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Magistrate Division** of District Court handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Community Property Income Rule (Civil Law Rule)
- Income from separate property is **community property**
- Different from California (American Rule)
- Affects trust funding and estate planning significantly
- Requires careful tracking of separate property income

### Simple Probate (UPC)
- Uniform Probate Code adopted
- Informal probate reduces cost and complexity
- Small estate procedures efficient
- Makes probate less burdensome

### Agricultural Considerations
- Farm and ranch planning important
- Special use valuation (IRC § 2032A) may apply
- Family farm succession planning critical
- Conservation easements may provide benefits

### Planning Considerations
- Community property provides automatic step-up in basis for both halves
- Income from separate property becomes community - plan accordingly
- Simple probate may reduce need for revocable trust
- Consider community property trust for clear classification
- TOD deeds useful for real property outside trust
- Coordinate federal estate tax and community property planning

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Magistrate division of district court handles probate

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement
- Community property considerations may apply to contents

### Community Property Note
- Box contents may be characterized as community or separate property
- Proper documentation important
- Income from separate property in box may be community

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document community vs. separate property in box
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider adding fiduciary as authorized signer
