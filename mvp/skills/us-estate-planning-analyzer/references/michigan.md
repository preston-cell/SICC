# Michigan

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **Lady Bird Deed (Enhanced Life Estate)** available
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Homestead exemption with unique features
- Strong retirement account protections

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Michigan Compiled Laws (MCL) Chapter 700 (Estates and Protected Individuals Code - EPIC)

### Execution Requirements (MCL 700.2502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (MCL 700.2504)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (MCL 700.2502)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- Date recommended but not required
- No witness requirement

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness allowed
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: MCL 700.7101 et seq. (Michigan Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (MCL 700.7402)
- Michigan Trust Code adopted (based on UTC)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Michigan Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (MCL 700.7602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (MCL 700.7703a)
- Michigan allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor/protector can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (MCL 700.7820a)
- Authorized under Michigan law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Requires trustee discretionary power

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: MCL 700.5501 et seq.

### Execution Requirements (MCL 700.5501)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed** OR **notarized**
- Both recommended for real estate
- Statutory form available

### Durability (MCL 700.5501)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (MCL 700.5501)
- Michigan provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties should accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations

### Third Party Acceptance (MCL 700.5507)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: MCL 700.5506 et seq. (Patient Advocate Designation)

### Types Available
- Patient Advocate Designation
- Living Will (limited recognition)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Patient Advocate Requirements (MCL 700.5506)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Spouse or heir
  - Attending physician
  - Healthcare provider employee
  - Patient advocate named

### Patient Advocate Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions
- Mental health treatment decisions (if granted)

### Michigan POLST
- Available in Michigan
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate
- Michigan does not have comprehensive default surrogate statute
- Patient advocate designation strongly recommended
- Guardian appointment may be needed

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (repealed 1993)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

## Medicaid Planning (Michigan Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Michigan-Specific Programs
- **Michigan Medicaid** administered by MDHHS
- **MI Choice Waiver** for home-based services
- **PACE** programs available
- Estate recovery program active

### Lady Bird Deed and Medicaid
- **Lady Bird Deed does NOT trigger Medicaid transfer penalty**
- Property transfers at death without 5-year look-back issue
- Excellent Medicaid planning tool
- Retains homestead exemption during life

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $9,500-10,500/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**MCL 600.6023**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$40,475** (indexed for inflation annually)
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Additional exemptions for personal property

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner (or buying under land contract)
- Automatic protection (no filing required)
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Additional Exemptions
- Personal property exemptions (tools, household goods)
- Vehicle exemption
- Retirement account protections

## Lady Bird Deed (Enhanced Life Estate)
**Common Law - Widely Recognized in Michigan**

### Features
- Retains **full control** during grantor's lifetime
- Grantor can sell, mortgage, or revoke without beneficiary consent
- Transfers property at death **without probate**
- **Does NOT trigger Medicaid transfer penalty**
- Property receives step-up in basis at death

### Advantages
- Avoids probate
- No Medicaid penalty
- Retains homestead exemption
- Can be revoked or changed
- No gift tax consequences during life
- Step-up in basis at death

### Requirements
- Must be in writing
- Must be signed by grantor
- Must be notarized (acknowledged)
- Must be recorded with Register of Deeds
- Must use proper enhanced life estate language

### Limitations
- Subject to creditors' claims at death
- Medicaid estate recovery may apply
- Only for Michigan real property
- Must use proper legal language

### Comparison to TOD Deed
- Lady Bird Deed more flexible
- Grantor retains more control
- Medicaid advantage significant
- Both avoid probate

## Transfer on Death Deed
**MCL 565.401 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime

### Requirements
- Must be in writing
- Must be signed by owner
- Must be acknowledged (notarized)
- Must be recorded before death
- Must designate beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- May not have same Medicaid advantages as Lady Bird Deed

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (MCL 700.3301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by register
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (MCL 700.3401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (MCL 700.3982)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$25,000** in personal property
- 28-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Proceedings (MCL 700.3987)
- For small estates
- Simplified court process
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from publication

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Court** handles estates
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Lady Bird Deed
- Widely used in Michigan
- Excellent Medicaid planning tool
- Avoids probate without Medicaid penalty
- More flexible than TOD Deed

### No Death Taxes
- No state estate or inheritance tax
- Simplifies planning
- Only federal considerations

### Strong Retirement Protections
- Qualified retirement accounts protected
- IRAs have substantial protection
- Protections apply during life and at death

### Planning Considerations
- Lady Bird Deed for real property Medicaid planning
- No state death tax simplifies planning
- Patient advocate designation essential (no default surrogate)
- Consider trust for privacy and multi-property estates
- Strong retirement account protections

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access rights
- Without joint renter: letters of authority from probate court required
- Bank may allow limited examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative access with court appointment

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access
- No automatic freeze upon death of co-renter
- Contents immediately accessible
- Consider implications for Medicaid planning with Lady Bird Deeds

### Best Practices
- Avoid storing original will in safe deposit box
- Keep copy of Lady Bird Deed in accessible location
- Document box contents
- Inform personal representative of box location

## State Charitable Incentives

### Limited State Credits
- Michigan has limited charitable tax credits currently active
- Focus on federal charitable deduction
- No state death tax means federal planning primary focus

### College Savings Plan Deduction
- Deduction (not credit) for 529 plan contributions
- While not charitable, supports educational giving

### Estate Planning Implications
- No major state charitable credits currently active
- Focus on federal charitable planning strategies
- Lady Bird Deed can coordinate with charitable remainder planning
- Consider charitable lead trusts for Michigan real estate
