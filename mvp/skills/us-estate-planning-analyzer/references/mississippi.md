# Mississippi

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- Higher Medicaid asset limit ($4,000)
- Holographic wills recognized
- Chancery Court handles probate
- Rural homestead protection (160 acres)
- Lower cost of living affects asset thresholds

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Mississippi Code Title 91

### Execution Requirements (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-5-1)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by mark at direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 credible witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in presence of testator

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-7-7)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-5-1)
- **Recognized** if entirely in testator's handwriting
- Must be signed by testator
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Nuncupative Wills (Oral)
- Recognized in limited circumstances
- Only for personal property under $100
- Military exception for larger amounts
- Must have witnesses present

### Witness Requirements
- Must be credible witnesses
- Must sign in presence of testator
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Miss. Code Ann. § 91-8-101 et seq. (Mississippi Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-8-402)
- Mississippi Trust Code adopted (based on UTC)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Mississippi Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-8-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- Mississippi allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting
- Available under Mississippi law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Miss. Code Ann. § 87-3-101 et seq. (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (Miss. Code Ann. § 87-3-105)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (Miss. Code Ann. § 87-3-104)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (Miss. Code Ann. § 87-3-130)
- Mississippi provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (Miss. Code Ann. § 87-3-120)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Miss. Code Ann. § 41-41-201 et seq. (Uniform Health-Care Decisions Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Health-Care Directive (combined form)
- Individual Instruction (Living Will)
- Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Mental Health Directive

### Execution Requirements (Miss. Code Ann. § 41-41-205)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Person who signed on behalf of principal
  - Healthcare provider
  - Related to principal

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Mississippi POLST
- Available in Mississippi
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (Miss. Code Ann. § 41-41-211)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Adult grandchild
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Focus on federal estate tax planning for large estates
- Probate avoidance still valuable for privacy and efficiency
- No need for credit shelter trusts for state tax purposes

## Medicaid Planning (Mississippi Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$4,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Mississippi-Specific Programs
- **Mississippi Medicaid** administered by Division of Medicaid
- **Elderly and Disabled Waiver** for home-based services
- Estate recovery program active
- Higher asset limit provides more flexibility

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $5,000-6,000/month (verify current rate - lower cost state)

## Homestead Protection
**Miss. Code Ann. § 85-3-21 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$75,000** in value protected
- OR up to **160 acres** in any location
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Must be head of family

### Requirements
- Must be **primary residence**
- Must be owner
- Must be head of family
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens (with exceptions)
  - Purchase money obligations

### Agricultural Protection
- 160-acre exemption valuable for farmers
- Protects family agricultural operations
- No separate value limit for acreage

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not specifically authorized by statute** in Mississippi
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Administration (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-7-1 et seq.)
- Chancery Court handles probate (unique to MS)
- Executor/administrator appointed by court
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Small Estate Procedures (Miss. Code Ann. § 91-7-322)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Simplified process

### Muniment of Title
- Available for small estates with real property
- Limited court involvement
- Transfers title without full administration

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 90 days from first publication

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Chancery Court** handles probate (unique to Mississippi)
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction
- Chancery Court also handles equity matters

## Unique State Features

### Chancery Court System
- Mississippi uses Chancery Court for probate
- Different from most states using Probate Court
- Chancery Court also handles equity, family law
- May affect procedures and timelines

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $4,000 individual limit more generous
- Provides additional planning flexibility
- Lower cost of living means assets go further

### Agricultural State
- Rural homestead important (160 acres)
- Farm succession planning significant
- Consider agricultural entity structures

### Lower Cost Environment
- Lower Medicaid penalty divisor
- Lower property values in many areas
- Asset thresholds may feel more generous

### Planning Considerations
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Chancery Court for probate matters
- Higher Medicaid asset limit advantageous
- Rural homestead protection valuable
- Trust planning for probate avoidance recommended
- Consider agricultural succession planning

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from chancery court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative appointment needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate planning documents
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider naming PR as joint renter
