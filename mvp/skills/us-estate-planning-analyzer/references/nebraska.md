# Nebraska

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- **Has state inheritance tax** (top rate 18% - highest in nation)
- Common law property state
- County-collected inheritance tax
- Three-class system based on relationship
- Higher Medicaid asset limit ($4,000)
- Holographic wills recognized
- Uniform Probate Code adopted

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Nebraska Revised Statutes Chapter 30 (Nebraska Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-2327)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-2329)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-2328)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness does not invalidate will
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3801 et seq. (Nebraska Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3840)
- Nebraska Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Nebraska UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3860)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3872)
- Nebraska allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting
- Available under Nebraska law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4001 et seq. (Nebraska Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4005)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4004)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4041)
- Nebraska provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4020)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-4040)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3401 et seq. (Rights of the Terminally Ill Act)

### Types Available
- Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Living Will (Declaration)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements
**Power of Attorney for Health Care (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-3404)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** AND **notarized**

**Living Will (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 20-404)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** AND **notarized**

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Nebraska POST
- Available in Nebraska
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate
- Nebraska has default surrogate provisions
- Priority order for surrogates
- Healthcare POA strongly recommended

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Nebraska Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **Has state inheritance tax** (rates below)
- Tax collected by **counties**, not state
- Based on beneficiary's relationship to decedent

### Class 1 (Immediate Family)
- Spouse, children, parents, grandparents, siblings, grandchildren
- **Exemption**: $100,000
- **Rate**: 1% on amounts over exemption

### Class 2 (Remote Relatives)
- Aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews, their descendants
- **Exemption**: $40,000
- **Rate**: 11% on amounts over exemption

### Class 3 (All Others)
- Friends, cousins, unmarried partners, other non-relatives
- **Exemption**: $25,000
- **Rate**: 18% on amounts over exemption
- **HIGHEST inheritance tax rate in the United States**

### Filing Requirements
- Return filed with county court
- Due 12 months after death
- Each county collects its own inheritance tax
- Personal representative responsible for payment

### Planning Implications
- **18% rate for Class 3 is highest in US** - careful planning essential
- Immediate family only pays 1% - favorable
- Structure bequests to lower-rate classes when possible
- Consider trusts for Class 3 beneficiaries
- Life insurance owned by beneficiary may avoid tax
- Charitable bequests exempt from inheritance tax

## Medicaid Planning (Nebraska Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$4,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Nebraska-Specific Programs
- **Nebraska Medicaid** administered by DHHS
- **Aged and Disabled Waiver** for home-based services
- Estate recovery program active
- Higher asset limit provides flexibility

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $7,000-8,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Neb. Rev. Stat. § 40-101 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$60,000** in value protected
- OR up to **160 acres** in rural areas (2 lots in city)
- Applies to dwelling and land
- Must be head of household

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Must be head of family
- Declaration recommended but protection automatic

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Purchase money obligations

### Agricultural Protection
- 160-acre rural exemption valuable for farmers
- Protects family agricultural operations

## Transfer on Death Deed
**Neb. Rev. Stat. § 76-3401 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with register of deeds
- Must designate beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- **Subject to inheritance tax** based on beneficiary relationship

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-2413)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by registrar
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-2420)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 30-24,125)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 2 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **County Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Highest Inheritance Tax Rate
- 18% Class 3 rate is highest in nation
- Careful planning for non-family beneficiaries essential
- Consider trusts, life insurance strategies

### County-Based Collection
- Each county collects and retains inheritance tax
- Filed with county court
- Unique in US for county-level death tax

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $4,000 individual limit more generous
- Provides additional planning flexibility

### Planning Considerations
- **Structure bequests to lower-rate classes** when possible
- Use trusts for Class 3 beneficiaries
- Life insurance owned by beneficiary avoids inheritance tax
- Charitable bequests exempt
- Higher Medicaid limit advantageous
- Agricultural succession planning important

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from county court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement

### Inheritance Tax Note
- Nebraska has inheritance tax - document contents for tax reporting
- Spouse exempt; other rates vary by relationship (1%, 11%, or 18%)
- Contents subject to inheritance tax based on recipient

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document valuable contents for inheritance tax purposes
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider inheritance tax implications for non-spouse beneficiaries
