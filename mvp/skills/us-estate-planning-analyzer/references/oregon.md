# Oregon

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has state estate tax** (exemption $1M - LOWEST threshold tied with MA)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- No portability
- **No sales tax**
- Limited homestead protection
- No holographic wills

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Oregon Revised Statutes Chapter 112

### Execution Requirements (ORS 112.235)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (ORS 113.055)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Oregon
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be valid if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness does not invalidate will
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: ORS Chapter 130 (Oregon Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (ORS 130.175)
- Oregon Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Oregon UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (ORS 130.505)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (ORS 130.655)
- Oregon allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (ORS 130.715)
- Authorized under Oregon UTC
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: ORS Chapter 127 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (ORS 127.015)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (ORS 127.015)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (ORS 127.700)
- Oregon provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations

### Third Party Acceptance
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: ORS Chapter 127 (Advance Directive Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Directive (combined form)
- Healthcare Representative Appointment
- Living Will (Instructions)

### Execution Requirements (ORS 127.515)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Healthcare representative
  - Attending physician
  - Owner of care facility

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Oregon POLST
- Available in Oregon (state pioneered POLST)
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Bright green form

### Default Surrogate (ORS 127.635)
- If no advance directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Any adult relative
  - Close friend

## Estate Tax

### Oregon Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $1,000,000 (tied with MA for LOWEST in nation)
- **Not indexed** for inflation
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%

### Rate Structure (ORS 118.010)
| Taxable Estate | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $500,000 | 10% |
| $500,001 - $1M | 10.25% |
| $1M - $1.5M | 10.5% |
| $1.5M - $2M | 11% |
| $2M - $2.5M | 11.5% |
| $2.5M - $3M | 12% |
| $3M - $3.5M | 13% |
| $3.5M - $4M | 14% |
| $4M - $5M | 15% |
| Over $5M | 16% |

### No Cliff Effect (Unlike MA)
- Only excess over $1M exemption is taxed
- Graduated rates apply
- Still significant at low threshold

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Oregon Department of Revenue
- Must file if gross estate exceeds $1M

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- **$1M threshold is very low** - many middle-class estates affected
- Portland/metro real estate often exceeds threshold alone
- Credit shelter trusts critical for married couples
- ILIT essential for life insurance
- Aggressive lifetime gifting may be warranted
- Consider domicile change for very large estates

## Medicaid Planning (Oregon Health Plan)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Oregon-Specific Programs
- **Oregon Health Plan** is Oregon's Medicaid program
- **Coordinated Care Organizations** (CCOs)
- Home and Community Based Services
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $10,000-11,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**ORS 18.395, 18.428**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$40,000** in equity protected
- Up to **$50,000** for two owners
- One of lower protections nationally
- Some additional exemptions for 65+

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Limited Protection
- One of lower homestead exemptions nationally
- Consider other asset protection strategies

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not specifically authorized by statute** in Oregon
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Full Administration (ORS Chapter 113)
- Circuit Court handles probate
- Personal representative appointed
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Small Estate Procedures (ORS 114.505)
- **Summary administration** for estates under **$75,000** in personal property
- **$200,000** if surviving spouse is sole beneficiary
- No real property over $200,000
- Simplified procedures

### Affidavit Procedure (ORS 114.515)
- For small personal property claims
- 30-day waiting period after death

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-18 months typical
- Small estate: 3-6 months
- Complex estates: 18-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Circuit Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## No Sales Tax

### State Tax Environment
- **No state sales tax**
- State income tax applies
- Estate tax with $1M threshold
- No sales tax partially offsets estate tax burden

## Unique State Features

### Lowest Estate Tax Threshold
- $1M exemption tied for lowest in nation
- Many middle-class estates affected
- Portland/metro real estate often exceeds threshold
- Planning urgency for most homeowners

### No Sales Tax
- One of few states with no sales tax
- Partially offsets estate tax burden
- Affects overall tax planning

### Limited Homestead
- Only $40,000-$50,000 protection
- Consider trusts for asset protection
- Limited creditor protection

### Planning Considerations
- **$1M threshold requires aggressive planning**
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- ILIT for life insurance (critical)
- Consider lifetime gifting
- Domicile change for very large estates
- No TOD deed - use trusts for real property
- Limited homestead requires alternative protection

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from circuit court required
- Oregon allows limited access with death certificate to search for will
- Personal representative appointment needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement
- Common approach for married couples

### Estate Tax Considerations
- Oregon has estate tax with $1M threshold (lowest tier)
- Document box contents for estate tax purposes
- Valuation critical at low threshold

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents for estate tax purposes (low $1M threshold)
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider joint rental for estate planning convenience

## State Charitable Incentives

### Oregon Cultural Trust Tax Credit
- **100% credit** for contributions to the Oregon Cultural Trust
- Requires matching contribution to an Oregon cultural nonprofit first
- Individual: up to $500 credit
- Joint: up to $1,000 credit
- Very efficient - full credit, not deduction

### Oregon Charitable Checkoff Program
- Designate contributions through state tax return
- Supports various Oregon nonprofits
- Convenient giving mechanism

### Estate Planning Implications
- Cultural Trust credit offers 100% efficiency - highly valuable
- With low $1M estate tax threshold, charitable giving reduces exposure
- Charitable bequests directly reduce taxable estate below threshold
- Consider charitable remainder trusts to reduce estate below $1M
- Charitable lead trusts can pass assets to heirs with reduced tax
- Lifetime charitable giving before Oregon residency may preserve credits
- ILIT for life insurance removes from estate (critical at $1M threshold)
