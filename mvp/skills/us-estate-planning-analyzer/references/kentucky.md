# Kentucky

## Key Differences from Other States
- **State inheritance tax** (one of six states)
- No state estate tax
- Common law property state
- **Class-based inheritance tax** with rates up to 16%
- Dower and curtesy rights recognized
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Strong spousal protections

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Kentucky Revised Statutes (KRS) Chapter 394

### Execution Requirements (KRS 394.040)
- Testator must be **18+ years old** (or younger if married, in military, or emancipated)
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be **attested and subscribed** by **2 or more credible witnesses** in presence of testator

### Self-Proving Affidavit (KRS 394.225)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate
- Must be attached to will

### Holographic Wills (KRS 394.040)
- **Recognized** if entirely in testator's handwriting
- Must be signed by testator
- No witness requirement
- Must show testamentary intent
- Date recommended but not required

### Witness Requirements
- Must be credible witnesses (competent)
- Interested witness allowed but gift may be void
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Must sign in presence of testator

### Nuncupative (Oral) Wills (KRS 394.050)
- Recognized for personal property only
- Limited to $500 (non-military) or $1,000 (military)
- Requires 2 witnesses
- Must be proved within 6 months

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: KRS Chapter 386B (Kentucky Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (KRS 386B.4-020)
- Kentucky Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Kentucky UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon death

### Revocable Trusts (KRS 386B.6-020)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (KRS 386B.8-080)
- Kentucky allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Useful for investment management

### Trust Decanting (KRS 386B.8-165)
- Authorized under Kentucky law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Requires trustee discretionary power
- Notice requirements apply

### Community Property Trust (KRS 386B.10)
- Kentucky allows **opt-in community property trusts**
- Provides double step-up in basis benefit
- Must meet statutory requirements
- Unusual for non-community property state
- Effective for income tax planning

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: KRS Chapter 386B, Part 5

### Execution Requirements (KRS 386B.5-030)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Must clearly identify agent

### Durability (KRS 386B.5-010)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available (effective upon incapacity)

### Statutory Form (KRS 386B.5-110)
- Kentucky provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties (KRS 386B.5-050)
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (KRS 386B.5-040)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship
  - Delegate authority
  - Waive survivorship rights

### Third Party Acceptance (KRS 386B.5-080)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request agent certification
- Refusal may result in liability
- Good faith reliance protected

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: KRS Chapter 311 (Living Will Directive); KRS 311.625 et seq. (Healthcare Surrogate)

### Types Available
- Living Will Directive - KRS 311.622 et seq.
- Healthcare Surrogate Designation - KRS 311.625
- Combined Advance Directive

### Living Will Requirements (KRS 311.625)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Related by blood or marriage
  - Entitled to any portion of estate
  - The attending physician
  - Employee of the healthcare facility

### Healthcare Surrogate (KRS 311.625)
- May be included in living will
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed** OR **notarized**
- Surrogate must be adult

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Kentucky POST (KRS 311.625)
- Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment
- Available for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (KRS 311.631)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Nearest living relative
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Kentucky Inheritance Tax
- **State inheritance tax applies**
- **No state estate tax**
- Tax based on **relationship to decedent**
- Rates from **4% to 16%**
- Three classes of beneficiaries

### Beneficiary Classes and Rates (KRS 140.070)

**Class A - Exempt (No Tax)**
- Surviving spouse
- Children, stepchildren, adopted children
- Grandchildren and other lineal descendants
- Parents
- Grandparents
- Siblings

**Class B - Rates 4% to 16%**
- Nieces and nephews
- Half-nieces and half-nephews
- Daughters-in-law and sons-in-law
- Aunts and uncles

| Amount Over Exemption | Rate |
|----------------------|------|
| First $10,000 | 4% |
| $10,001 - $20,000 | 5% |
| $20,001 - $30,000 | 6% |
| Over $30,000 | 8% |

Class B exemption: $1,000

**Class C - Rates 6% to 16%**
- All other beneficiaries (unrelated persons, cousins, etc.)

| Amount Over Exemption | Rate |
|----------------------|------|
| First $10,000 | 6% |
| $10,001 - $20,000 | 8% |
| $20,001 - $30,000 | 10% |
| $30,001 - $40,000 | 12% |
| $40,001 - $50,000 | 14% |
| Over $50,000 | 16% |

Class C exemption: $500

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 18 months after death
- File with Kentucky Department of Revenue
- Personal representative responsible for filing
- Tax due upon filing

### Planning Implications
- Leave assets to Class A beneficiaries when possible
- Charitable bequests exempt
- Consider generation-skipping to Class A beneficiaries
- Life insurance payable to estate is taxable
- Trust distributions subject to inheritance tax

### No Estate Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

## Dower and Curtesy Rights
**One of Few States Still Recognizing**

### Dower (KRS 392.020)
- Surviving spouse entitled to **1/3** of deceased spouse's real property for life
- Applies to property owned during marriage
- Cannot be defeated by will alone
- Must be released or waived

### Curtesy
- Abolished in Kentucky
- Only dower applies to both genders

### Spousal Rights (KRS 392.020)
- **1/2** of real property if no children
- **1/3** of real property if children
- Can elect to take dower OR under the will
- Cannot be disinherited without valid waiver

### Planning Implications
- Spouse must join in deeds to convey clear title
- Prenuptial agreements can waive dower
- Trust planning must account for dower
- Joint tenancy avoids dower issues

## Medicaid Planning (Kentucky Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Kentucky-Specific Programs
- **Kentucky Medicaid** administered by Cabinet for Health and Family Services
- **Home and Community Based (HCB) Waiver**
- **Supports for Community Living (SCL)**
- **PACE** in limited areas
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $7,000-8,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**KRS 427.060**

### Exemption Amount
- **$5,000** equity protected
- One of lower exemptions in nation
- Applies to dwelling house and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be used as dwelling
- Only one homestead per family
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Purchase money obligations

### Additional Protections
- $3,000 exemption for personalty
- Tools of trade exemption
- Vehicle exemption (limited)

## Transfer on Death Deed
**KRS 382.135 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Must be **recorded before death** with county clerk
- Must designate grantee-beneficiary
- Must contain required statutory language

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Subject to inheritance tax
- Only for Kentucky real property

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Regular Administration (KRS 395)
- Court supervised process
- Personal representative appointed
- Bond typically required
- Inventory and accounting required

### Small Estate (Dispensing with Administration) (KRS 395.455)
- Estate under **$30,000** (personal property)
- 30-day waiting period
- Affidavit procedure
- No administration required

### Settlement Without Administration (KRS 395.460)
- Estate passes entirely to surviving spouse
- Simplified procedures
- Court approval required

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months

### Court Jurisdiction
- **District Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Inheritance Tax
- One of only six states with inheritance tax
- Class-based system benefits close relatives
- Significant planning consideration
- Charitable bequests exempt

### Dower Rights
- One of few states with dower
- Spouse cannot be disinherited from real property
- Must be addressed in all estate plans
- Prenuptial agreements important

### Community Property Trust Option
- Opt-in community property trust available
- Provides double step-up in basis
- Unusual for common law property state
- Valuable income tax planning tool

### Planning Considerations
- Inheritance tax planning critical
- Leave assets to Class A beneficiaries when possible
- Dower rights must be addressed
- TOD deeds subject to inheritance tax
- Revocable trusts don't avoid inheritance tax
- Consider charitable planning
- Life insurance planning (beneficiary designation affects tax)
- Community property trust for income tax benefits

### Agricultural Considerations
- Farm succession planning important
- Family farming operations common
- Agricultural valuation provisions
- Conservation easements available

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death (KRS 286.8-080)
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate
- Can search for will
- Inventory required for removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate
- Consider liability exposure

### Inheritance Tax Note
- Kentucky has inheritance tax
- Document valuable contents for tax reporting
- Class A beneficiaries (spouse, children, parents) exempt
- Other beneficiaries subject to tax

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document valuable contents for inheritance tax purposes
- Inform executor of location
- Consider inheritance tax implications for non-exempt beneficiaries
