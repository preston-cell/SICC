# New Jersey

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax (repealed 2018)
- **Has state inheritance tax** (top rate 16%)
- Common law property state
- Class-based inheritance tax system
- Close relatives (Class A) fully exempt
- No homestead exemption
- No holographic wills
- Surrogate's Court handles probate

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: New Jersey Revised Statutes Title 3B

### Execution Requirements (N.J.S.A. 3B:3-2)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (N.J.S.A. 3B:3-4)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in New Jersey
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be challenged

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: N.J.S.A. Title 3B, Chapter 31 (New Jersey Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (N.J.S.A. 3B:31-32)
- New Jersey Uniform Trust Code adopted (2016)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- New Jersey UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (N.J.S.A. 3B:31-42)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (N.J.S.A. 3B:31-68)
- New Jersey allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (N.J.S.A. 3B:31-72)
- Authorized under New Jersey UTC
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: N.J.S.A. 46:2B-8.1 et seq.

### Execution Requirements (N.J.S.A. 46:2B-8.9)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- All three required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (N.J.S.A. 46:2B-8.2)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (N.J.S.A. 46:2B-8.13)
- New Jersey provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Delegate authority

### Third Party Acceptance (N.J.S.A. 46:2B-8.4)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: N.J.S.A. 26:2H-53 et seq. (Advance Directives for Health Care Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Directive for Health Care (combined form)
- Proxy Directive (designating healthcare agent)
- Instruction Directive (living will)

### Execution Requirements (N.J.S.A. 26:2H-56)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Healthcare agent
  - Healthcare provider where patient is
  - Patient representative of facility

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### New Jersey POLST
- Available in New Jersey
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Pink form

### Default Surrogate (N.J.S.A. 26:2H-64)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Adult grandchild
  - Close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### No Estate Tax
- **Estate tax repealed** January 1, 2018
- Prior exemption was $675,000 (very low)
- Repeal was significant planning improvement

### New Jersey Inheritance Tax
- **State inheritance tax still applies**
- Tax based on beneficiary relationship to decedent
- Four classes (A, C, D, E - no Class B)
- Rates range from 0% to 16%

### Class A (Exempt - No Tax)
- Surviving spouse or domestic partner
- Children (including stepchildren and adopted)
- Parents
- Grandparents
- Grandchildren and great-grandchildren
- **No inheritance tax** for Class A

### Class C (11-16%)
- Siblings
- Son-in-law, daughter-in-law
- **Exemption**: $25,000
- **Rates**: 11-16% graduated

### Class C Rate Schedule
| Taxable Amount | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $25,000 | Exempt |
| $25,001 - $1,100,000 | 11% |
| $1,100,001 - $1,400,000 | 13% |
| $1,400,001 - $1,700,000 | 14% |
| Over $1,700,000 | 16% |

### Class D (15-16%)
- All other individuals not in A, C, or E
- Nieces, nephews, cousins, friends, unmarried partners
- **No exemption** ($0)
- **Rates**: 15-16%

### Class D Rate Schedule
| Taxable Amount | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $700,000 | 15% |
| Over $700,000 | 16% |

### Class E (Exempt)
- Qualified charities
- Religious organizations
- Educational institutions
- **No inheritance tax**

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 8 months after death
- Extension available
- File with NJ Division of Taxation
- Personal representative must file and pay

### Planning Implications
- **Class A is fully exempt** - bequests to spouse/children tax-free
- **Class D has no exemption** - plan carefully for non-relatives
- Siblings (Class C) have better treatment than friends
- Use trusts for Class D beneficiaries
- Life insurance owned by beneficiary may avoid tax
- Charitable bequests exempt (Class E)

## Medicaid Planning (NJ FamilyCare)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### New Jersey-Specific Programs
- **NJ FamilyCare** is New Jersey's Medicaid program
- **MLTSS** (Managed Long-Term Services and Supports)
- Home and Community Based Services
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $12,000-13,000/month (verify current rate - high cost state)

## Homestead Protection
- **No general homestead exemption** in New Jersey
- Very limited creditor protection for residence
- One of few states without meaningful homestead protection
- Tenancy by entirety provides some protection for married couples

### Alternative Protections
- Tenancy by entirety for married couples
- Trust ownership
- Retirement account protections
- Life insurance protections

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not authorized** in New Jersey
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Probate Administration (N.J.S.A. 3B:10-1 et seq.)
- **Surrogate's Court** in each county handles probate
- Executor/administrator appointed by Surrogate
- Inventory required within 60 days
- Accounting required

### Informal Probate
- Available for uncontested estates
- Surrogate can issue letters without formal hearing
- Most common form

### Formal Probate
- Required if will contested
- Superior Court involvement
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (N.J.S.A. 3B:10-3)
- **Summary administration** for estates under **$20,000** in personal property
- Affidavit procedure available for very small estates
- Simplified procedures

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24+ months
- Creditor claim period: 9 months from appointment

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Surrogate's Court** handles routine probate
- **Superior Court** for contested matters
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Inheritance Tax Without Estate Tax
- Estate tax repealed in 2018
- Inheritance tax remains
- Class-based system
- Immediate family exempt

### No Homestead Protection
- One of few states without meaningful homestead exemption
- Alternative strategies needed for asset protection
- Tenancy by entirety is primary protection

### Class D No Exemption
- Non-family beneficiaries (Class D) have no exemption
- Taxed from first dollar at 15-16%
- Plan carefully for unmarried partners, friends

### High Cost State
- Higher Medicaid penalty divisor
- Higher property values
- Higher income requirements

### Planning Considerations
- Structure bequests to Class A (exempt) beneficiaries when possible
- Use trusts for Class D beneficiaries
- Life insurance for inheritance tax liquidity
- No homestead - use tenancy by entirety, trusts
- No TOD deed - use trusts for real property

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from surrogate's court required
- New Jersey allows examination with death certificate to locate will
- Executor/administrator needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without surrogate court involvement

### Inheritance Tax Note
- New Jersey has inheritance tax - document contents for tax reporting
- Class A (spouse, children, parents, grandparents) exempt
- Other beneficiaries subject to tax up to 16%

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document valuable contents for inheritance tax purposes
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider inheritance tax for non-Class A beneficiaries
