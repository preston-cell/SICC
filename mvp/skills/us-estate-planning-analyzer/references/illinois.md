# Illinois

## Key Differences from Other States
- **State estate tax** with $4 million exemption
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- No portability for state estate tax exemption
- Generous homestead exemption ($15,000)
- Transfer on Death Instrument available
- Strong creditor protections for retirement accounts
- Cook County has unique probate procedures

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: 755 ILCS 5/ (Illinois Probate Act of 1975)

### Execution Requirements (755 ILCS 5/4-3)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind and memory**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be **attested in presence of testator** by **2 or more credible witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in presence of testator

### Self-Proving Affidavit (755 ILCS 5/6-4)
- Available and strongly recommended
- Testator and witnesses execute affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate
- Must be attached to or follow will

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Illinois
- All wills must be witnessed
- Even wills valid in other states may face challenges

### Witness Requirements
- Must be credible witnesses
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not invalidate will but may affect gift

### Foreign Wills
- Wills valid where executed are valid in Illinois
- Must meet Illinois formalities OR state of execution formalities

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: 760 ILCS 3/ (Illinois Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (760 ILCS 3/402)
- Illinois Trust Code adopted (effective 2020)
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property (Statute of Frauds)

### Trust Administration
- Illinois Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies (760 ILCS 5/5)
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Notice to beneficiaries required
- Accounting duties apply

### Revocable Trusts (760 ILCS 3/602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death
- 2-year creditor claim period after death

### Directed Trusts (760 ILCS 3/808)
- Illinois allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Popular for investment management

### Trust Decanting (760 ILCS 3/1301)
- Authorized under Illinois Trust Code
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee with discretionary distribution power can decant
- Various restrictions apply

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: 755 ILCS 45/ (Illinois Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (755 ILCS 45/2-4)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be signed by **one witness**
- Must be **notarized**
- Statutory form strongly recommended

### Statutory Form (755 ILCS 45/3-3)
- Illinois statutory short form widely accepted
- Third parties generally must accept
- Other forms valid but may face resistance
- Statutory form provides clear powers

### Durability (755 ILCS 45/2-6)
- Durable unless document states otherwise (default durable)
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available but not recommended

### Agent Duties (755 ILCS 45/2-7)
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require specific grant:
  - Make gifts (limited without express authority)
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Disclaim property

### Acceptance Requirements (755 ILCS 45/2-8)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request agent certification
- Refusal may result in liability
- 30-day deadline to accept or reject

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: 755 ILCS 35/ (Illinois Living Will Act); 755 ILCS 40/ (Health Care Surrogate Act)

### Types Available
- Declaration (Living Will) - 755 ILCS 35/
- Power of Attorney for Health Care - 755 ILCS 45/4
- Mental Health Treatment Declaration - 755 ILCS 43/

### Living Will Requirements (755 ILCS 35/3)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be related by blood or marriage
- Cannot be entitled to estate
- Cannot be attending physician

### Power of Attorney for Health Care (755 ILCS 45/4-5)
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be signed by **one witness**
- Must be **notarized** (notary cannot be agent)
- Witness cannot be agent

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records (HIPAA authorization included)
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions
- Mental health treatment authority (if granted)

### POLST (Practitioner Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment)
- Available in Illinois
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Bright pink form
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Healthcare Surrogate Act (755 ILCS 40/)
- Provides default surrogates if no POA
- Priority: guardian, spouse, adult children, parents, siblings, etc.
- Allows decisions without advance directive

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Illinois Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $4 million (not indexed)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%
- Graduated rates from 0.8% to 16%
- Tax calculated on Illinois portion of estate

### Rate Structure (35 ILCS 405/3)
| Taxable Estate | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $40,000 | 0.8% |
| $40,001 - $90,000 | 1.6% |
| $90,001 - $140,000 | 2.4% |
| $140,001 - $240,000 | 3.2% |
| ... | ... |
| Over $10.04M | 16% |

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Illinois Attorney General
- Must file if federal return required OR Illinois estate exceeds $4M

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- Gap between Illinois ($4M) and federal ($13.99M) exemption creates planning opportunity
- Credit shelter/bypass trusts common for married couples
- QTIP trusts for estate tax deferral
- No portability means both spouses should use exemptions
- Consider Illinois disclaimer trusts

## Medicaid Planning (Illinois Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts ($15,000 limit)
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Illinois-Specific Programs
- **Illinois Medicaid** administered by HFS
- **Community Care Program** for home-based services
- **Supportive Living Program** (assisted living alternative)
- Estate recovery program active (MERP)
- **ABLE accounts** available

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $9,500-10,500/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**735 ILCS 5/12-901 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- **$15,000** equity protected (individual)
- **$30,000** for married couples (joint homestead)
- Applies to lot and buildings used as residence

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be occupied as home
- Only one homestead per family
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens (prior to homestead)
  - Child support/alimony
  - Valid liens before homestead established

### Proceeds from Sale
- Proceeds exempt for 1 year
- Must be reinvested in new homestead
- Same dollar limits apply

## Transfer on Death Instrument
**765 ILCS 27/ (Illinois Residential Real Property Transfer on Death Instrument Act)**

### Features
- Transfer residential real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Limited to **residential real estate** (1-4 units)

### Requirements
- Must use **statutory form**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must contain required statutory language

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- **Only residential property** (not commercial/agricultural)
- Property must be located in Illinois

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording revocation instrument
- Subsequent TODI supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Independent Administration (755 ILCS 5/28-1)
- Default in Illinois (unless will prohibits or heir objects)
- Minimal court supervision
- Representative acts without court approval
- Most estates administered independently
- Must provide notice to heirs

### Supervised Administration
- Required if independent administration not authorized
- Full court supervision
- Court approval for most actions
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Affidavit (755 ILCS 5/25-1)
- Personal property under **$100,000**
- 30-day waiting period after death
- Affidavit procedure (no court filing)
- Cannot include real estate

### Summary Administration
- For estates under certain thresholds
- Simplified procedures
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Independent administration: 6-12 months typical
- Supervised administration: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months from first publication

### Cook County Considerations
- Largest probate court in state
- Specific local rules apply
- May have longer processing times
- Electronic filing available

## Unique State Features

### No Portability for State Estate Tax
- Unlike federal estate tax, no portability
- Both spouses must use their $4M exemption
- Credit shelter trust planning essential for married couples
- QTIP/QDOT planning important

### Gap Planning
- $4M Illinois exemption vs. $13.99M federal exemption
- Creates significant planning opportunities
- Illinois-only credit shelter trusts
- Consider gift planning to reduce Illinois estate

### Retirement Account Protections
- IRAs and qualified plans protected from creditors
- 100% exempt from judgment creditors
- Strong protection during life and at death

### Land Trusts (765 ILCS 410/)
- Illinois land trusts available
- Beneficial interest is personal property
- Privacy benefits
- Common in Chicago area real estate

### Planning Considerations
- State estate tax planning critical for estates over $4M
- Use both spouses' Illinois exemptions
- Consider QTIP trusts for portability of income
- Land trusts for real estate privacy
- Independent administration reduces costs
- TODI for residential property probate avoidance
- Strong retirement account protections

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death (205 ILCS 605/)
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters of office required
- Bank may permit limited examination with death certificate
- Can search for will, burial instructions, life insurance
- Inventory required before removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate
- Consider liability exposure

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider adding fiduciary as deputy

## State Charitable Incentives

### No State Charitable Tax Credits
- Illinois does not offer state charitable tax credits
- Federal charitable deduction applies
- No state gift tax

### Estate Planning Implications
- Charitable giving relies on federal deduction
- Charitable remainder trusts for income/estate planning
- Private foundations subject to federal rules only
- Consider charitable lead trusts for estate tax reduction
