# Alaska

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- **Opt-in community property** available via community property trust/agreement
- Strong asset protection trust laws (Domestic Asset Protection Trust - DAPT)
- No state income tax
- Unique permanent fund dividend considerations
- Dynasty trusts permitted (no rule against perpetuities)
- One of the most favorable jurisdictions for trust-based planning

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Alaska Statutes Title 13 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (AS 13.12.502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another in testator's conscious presence at testator's direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing testator's signature or acknowledgment

### Self-Proving Affidavit (AS 13.12.504)
- Available and recommended for smooth probate
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Eliminates need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (AS 13.12.502)
- **Recognized** if material portions in testator's handwriting and signed by testator
- No witness requirement for holographic wills
- Date not required but recommended

### Choice of Law
- Will valid if executed in compliance with Alaska law OR law of state/country where executed

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Alaska Statutes Title 13, Chapter 36

### Trust Creation
- Alaska Trust Act is very favorable for trust creation
- Must have identifiable trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries
- Settlor must have capacity

### Alaska Asset Protection Trust (DAPT) (AS 13.36.310)
- **One of the first states** to allow DAPTs (1997)
- Settlor can be discretionary beneficiary
- **4-year statute of limitations** for creditor claims (reduced from original 10 years)
- Must have at least one Alaska resident trustee (or qualified Alaska trust company)
- Some Alaska-situs assets required
- Must be irrevocable
- Independent trustee must have absolute discretion over distributions

### DAPT Requirements
- Must be governed by Alaska law
- At least one qualified trustee in Alaska
- Some trust assets must be deposited or held in Alaska
- Settlor must not be trustee
- Transfer must not be fraudulent
- Trust must contain spendthrift provision

### Dynasty Trusts (AS 13.36.320)
- **No rule against perpetuities** for trusts created after 1997
- Trusts can last indefinitely (1,000 years permitted)
- Popular jurisdiction for multi-generational wealth transfer
- Avoids generation-skipping transfer tax at each generation
- Protects assets from beneficiaries' creditors indefinitely

### Community Property Trusts (AS 34.77)
- Both spouses must be grantors and beneficiaries
- Provides double step-up in basis benefit at first death
- Must be properly structured to maintain CP character

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: AS 13.26

### Execution Requirements
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **notarized**
- Must be **witnessed** (recommended)
- Durable POA requires specific durability language: "This power of attorney is not affected by subsequent disability or incapacity of the principal"

### Statutory Form
- Alaska provides statutory form (AS 13.26.332)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Recommended to use statutory form for easier acceptance

### Durability (AS 13.26.344)
- POA is **NOT durable by default** - must include durability language
- Springing POA available (effective upon incapacity)

### Termination
- Terminates upon principal's death
- Terminates upon principal's incapacity (unless durable)
- Can be revoked by competent principal at any time

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: AS 13.52 (Health Care Decisions Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Health Care Directive (combined document)
- Living Will
- Healthcare Power of Attorney
- Mental Health Treatment Declaration

### Execution Requirements (AS 13.52.010)
- Must be signed by principal
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be healthcare provider or their employee
- Witnesses cannot be person designated as agent

### Witness Restrictions
- Cannot be treating healthcare provider
- Cannot be employee of treating healthcare provider
- Cannot be designated agent
- Should not be related by blood, marriage, or adoption (recommended)

### Effectiveness
- Living will effective when:
  1. Patient cannot communicate healthcare decisions, AND
  2. Two physicians determine terminal condition or permanent unconsciousness

### POLST (Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment)
- Available in Alaska
- Portable medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Signed by physician and patient/representative

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax**
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

## Opt-In Community Property

### Community Property Agreement (AS 34.77)
- Spouses can agree to treat property as community property
- Must be in writing and signed by both spouses
- Creates community property ownership
- Can be revoked by written agreement of both spouses

### Community Property Trust (AS 34.77.100)
- Alternative method to create community property
- Both spouses must be grantors and beneficiaries
- Provides double step-up in basis benefit at first death
- Trust must expressly state community property character

### Benefits
- **Full step-up in basis** on both halves of community property at first spouse's death
- Can provide significant income tax savings
- Particularly valuable for appreciated assets
- Important for couples who moved from community property states

### Requirements for Basis Step-Up
- Property must be held as community property at death
- Proper documentation essential
- Must maintain community property character in trust

## Medicaid Planning (Alaska Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- Income limit: approximately $2,829/month for individuals

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings
- Household goods
- Prepaid burial contracts
- Life insurance with face value under $1,500

### Alaska-Specific Considerations
- **Higher cost of living** affects penalty divisor
- Remote location considerations for care options
- **Permanent Fund Dividend counts as income**
- Limited nursing facility options in some areas
- Home and community-based waiver programs available
- Tribal health services may supplement Medicaid

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $15,000-18,000/month (verify current rate - higher than most states due to cost of living)

## Homestead Protection (AS 09.38.010)
- Up to **$72,900** exemption for primary residence
- Must be **recorded** to be effective
- Automatic for head of household
- Protects against most creditors
- Does not protect against mortgages, property taxes, or mechanic's liens

## Probate Procedures

### Small Estate Procedures (AS 13.16.680)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under $100,000 in personal property
- No real property can be included
- 30-day waiting period after death
- Claimant must be entitled to property

### Summary Administration (AS 13.16.690)
- Available for estates under $200,000
- Simplified court procedure
- Reduced bond requirements

### Formal vs. Informal Probate
- **Informal probate** available for uncontested estates
- Formal probate required if will contested or disputes exist
- Superior Court has probate jurisdiction

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typically
- Formal probate: 1-2 years or longer

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from superior court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Alaska Uniform Probate Code governs administration

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement
- Important for residents with assets in remote locations

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate planning attorney
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider accessibility issues for remote Alaska residents
- Name successor trustee/executor as authorized signer

## Unique State Features

### Dynasty Trusts
- No rule against perpetuities for trusts created after 1997
- Trusts can last up to 1,000 years
- Popular jurisdiction for multi-generational wealth transfer
- GST tax exemption can be leveraged for multiple generations

### Asset Protection Trusts
- One of first states to allow DAPTs (1997)
- Strong creditor protection
- 4-year lookback for fraudulent transfers
- No exception creditors (unlike some other DAPT states)
- Self-settled spendthrift protection available

### No State Income Tax
- No income tax on trust income for Alaska trusts
- Attractive for out-of-state settlors (with limitations)
- Complete trust income earned in Alaska is state-tax-free
- Important for trust situs planning

### Permanent Fund Dividend
- Annual payment to Alaska residents
- Counts as income for Medicaid eligibility
- Can be assigned to trust (with limitations)
- Plan for PFD in Medicaid spend-down calculations

### Planning Opportunities
- Establish Alaska DAPT for asset protection
- Use community property trust for step-up in basis
- Dynasty trust for multi-generational planning
- Combine federal and Alaska planning for maximum benefit
- Consider Alaska trust for out-of-state clients
- No state income tax on Alaska trust income
