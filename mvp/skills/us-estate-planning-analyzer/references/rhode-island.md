# Rhode Island

## Key Differences from Other States
- **Has state estate tax** (exemption ~$1.8M, indexed)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **Cliff effect** like Massachusetts
- Exemption indexed for inflation (unusual)
- No holographic wills
- Probate court in each city/town

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Rhode Island General Laws Title 33

### Execution Requirements (R.I. Gen. Laws § 33-5-5)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by mark/another at direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 competent witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (R.I. Gen. Laws § 33-5-5.1)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Rhode Island
- All wills must be formally witnessed
- Out-of-state holographic wills may be valid if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: R.I. Gen. Laws Chapter 18-4

### Trust Creation
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts
- Rhode Island allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: R.I. Gen. Laws Chapter 18-16 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (R.I. Gen. Laws § 18-16-105)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (R.I. Gen. Laws § 18-16-104)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (R.I. Gen. Laws § 18-16-301)
- Rhode Island provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (R.I. Gen. Laws § 18-16-201)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (R.I. Gen. Laws § 18-16-120)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: R.I. Gen. Laws Chapter 23-4.11 (Rights of the Terminally Ill Act)

### Types Available
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Living Will (Declaration)
- Combined Advance Directive

### Execution Requirements
**Healthcare POA (R.I. Gen. Laws § 23-4.10-2)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Notarization not required but recommended

**Living Will (R.I. Gen. Laws § 23-4.11-3)**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant**
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Attending physician
  - Related to declarant

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Rhode Island POLST/MOLST
- Available in Rhode Island
- Medical Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Bright pink form

### Default Surrogate (R.I. Gen. Laws § 23-4.11-7)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Nearest adult relative

## Estate Tax

### Rhode Island Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $1,802,431 (2025, indexed for inflation)
- **Indexed** for CPI-U annually
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%

### CLIFF EFFECT (CRITICAL)
- **Entire estate taxed** once threshold exceeded
- Similar to Massachusetts approach
- Small amount over exemption triggers tax on entire estate
- Creates planning urgency near threshold
- Example: $1,850,000 estate pays tax on full $1,850,000

### Rate Structure (R.I. Gen. Laws § 44-22-1.1)
| Taxable Estate | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 - $100,000 | 0.8% |
| $100,001 - $500,000 | Graduated |
| Over $500,000 | Up to 16% |

*Uses federal credit computation table*

### Indexed Exemption
- Adjusts annually for inflation (CPI-U)
- Rare among estate tax states
- Provides ongoing protection
- Started at $850,000, now over $1.8M

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Rhode Island Division of Taxation
- Must file if gross estate exceeds exemption

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- **Cliff effect creates planning urgency** near threshold
- Small excess over exemption triggers full tax
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples (no portability)
- Lifetime gifting to stay below exemption
- Consider domicile if choosing between RI and no-tax state
- Indexed exemption provides some inflation protection

## Medicaid Planning (Rhode Island Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$4,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Rhode Island-Specific Programs
- **RI Medicaid** administered by EOHHS
- **Home and Community Care** waiver
- **PACE** (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly)
- Higher individual asset limit ($4,000) more generous
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $10,000-11,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**R.I. Gen. Laws § 9-26-4.1**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$500,000** in equity protected
- Strong protection
- Applies to dwelling and land

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owner
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Strong Protection
- $500,000 exemption provides substantial coverage
- Higher than many northeastern states
- Valuable for retirement planning

## Transfer on Death Deed
- **Not available** in Rhode Island
- Use revocable trusts for probate avoidance
- Joint ownership with right of survivorship available
- Consider trust ownership for real property

## Probate Procedures

### Regular Probate (R.I. Gen. Laws § 33-7)
- Probate court in each city/town handles estates
- Executor/administrator appointed
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required

### Small Estate Procedures (R.I. Gen. Laws § 33-24)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$15,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Informal Administration
- Available for uncontested estates
- Less court supervision
- Faster process

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 6 months from publication

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Probate Court** in each city/town handles estates
- City/town where decedent resided has jurisdiction
- Municipal court system

## Unique State Features

### Cliff Effect
- Entire estate taxed if over exemption
- Creates planning urgency near threshold
- Unlike gradual tax in most states
- Same approach as Massachusetts

### Indexed Exemption
- Annual CPI-U adjustment (rare)
- Provides inflation protection
- Started at $850,000, now over $1.8M
- Unusual among estate tax states

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $4,000 individual limit more generous than most states
- Provides some additional flexibility
- Still quite low for planning purposes

### Proximity to Massachusetts
- Consider domicile election
- Both states have estate tax with cliff effect
- MA has lower exemption but no indexing
- RI indexed exemption may be preferable

### Planning Considerations
- Cliff effect requires staying below exemption
- Credit shelter trusts essential (no portability)
- Lifetime gifting to reduce estate
- Strong homestead ($500,000) provides protection
- Higher Medicaid limit provides slight flexibility
- Consider MA vs. RI domicile for larger estates

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate to locate will
- Executor/administrator needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement
- Common approach for married couples

### Estate Tax Considerations
- Rhode Island has estate tax ($1.8M threshold, indexed)
- Document valuable contents for estate tax purposes
- Cliff effect means accurate valuation critical

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document contents for estate tax reporting
- Inform executor of box location
- Consider joint rental for estate planning convenience

## State Charitable Incentives

### Limited State Credits
- Rhode Island has limited state-specific charitable credits
- Federal charitable deduction applies
- Focus on federal planning for charitable giving

### Estate Planning Implications
- Charitable bequests reduce taxable estate below cliff threshold
- Can help avoid cliff effect entirely
- Federal charitable deductions available
- No specific state credits currently
