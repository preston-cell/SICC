# Alabama

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax (eliminated effective January 1, 2005)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law (separate property) state
- Homestead exemption unlimited for head of household up to 160 acres
- Holographic wills NOT recognized
- Uses traditional elective share (not augmented estate approach)
- Recent Small Estates Act revision (effective October 1, 2025)

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code Title 43, Chapter 8

### Execution Requirements (Ala. Code § 43-8-131)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing** (typed or handwritten)
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by someone in testator's presence at their direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in presence of testator
- Witnesses must be generally competent (interested witnesses allowed but not recommended)

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Ala. Code § 43-8-132)
- Testator and witnesses execute affidavits before notary public
- Creates conclusive presumption of proper execution
- Can be probated without witness testimony unless fraud/forgery alleged
- **Highly recommended** to streamline probate

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Alabama
- Handwritten wills valid ONLY if they meet standard execution requirements (2 witnesses)

### Choice of Law (Ala. Code § 43-8-135)
- Will executed in compliance with Alabama law OR law of state where executed is valid

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code Title 19, Chapter 3B (Alabama Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Ala. Code § 19-3B-402)
- Alabama has adopted the **Uniform Trust Code**
- Must have identifiable trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries
- Settlor must have capacity

### Trust Administration
- **Limited court involvement** (§ 19-3B-201)
- **Prudent Investor Rule** applies (§ 19-3B-901)
- Trustee must review and reposition assets within reasonable time after acceptance

### Revocable Trusts (Ala. Code § 19-3B-602)
- Commonly used for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime

### Remedies for Breach (Ala. Code § 19-3B-1001)
- Courts may void trustee acts
- May impose liens or constructive trusts
- May trace and recover wrongfully disposed property

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code Title 26, Chapter 1A (Alabama Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **dated**
- **Must be notarized** (signature acknowledged before notary public)

### Durability (Ala. Code § 26-1A-104)
- Powers of attorney are **durable by default** unless document states otherwise
- Remains effective after principal's incapacitation

### Effective Date (Ala. Code § 26-1A-109)
- Effective immediately upon signing unless document specifies otherwise (springing POA)

### Termination (Ala. Code § 26-1A-110)
- Automatically terminates upon principal's death
- May be revoked by competent principal at any time
- **Divorce provision**: If spouse named as agent, authority automatically terminates when either party files for divorce

### Statutory Form (Ala. Code § 26-1A-301)
- Alabama provides statutory form (recommended but not required)

## Healthcare Directive / Advance Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code Title 22, Chapter 8A (Termination of Life-Support Procedures)

### Types Available
- Living Will
- Health Care Proxy
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care
- Combination documents

### Execution Requirements (Ala. Code § 22-8A-4)
- **Age requirement**: Must be at least **19 years old**
- Must be **competent** and able to make decisions
- Must be witnessed by **2 adults**

### Witness Restrictions
- Witnesses must be at least 19 years old
- Cannot be related by blood, adoption, or marriage to declarant
- Cannot be entitled to any part of declarant's estate
- Cannot be directly responsible for paying medical care

### Health Care Proxy Requirements
- Proxy must be at least 19 years of age
- Cannot be health care provider or employee (unless related to declarant)

### Effectiveness
- Becomes effective when:
  1. Attending physician determines declarant cannot understand/direct treatment, AND
  2. Two physicians document terminal illness/injury OR permanent unconsciousness

### Pregnancy Exception
- Advance directive has **NO EFFECT** during pregnancy

### Conflicting Directives
- If both living will and proxy exist, **proxy decisions take precedence**

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code Title 40, Chapter 15 (repealed for deaths after 12/31/2004)

### State Taxes
- **No state estate tax** (eliminated effective January 1, 2005)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**

### Federal Estate Tax
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Fiduciary Income Tax (Ala. Code § 40-18-29(a))
- Alabama Fiduciary Income Tax still active
- Resident trusts with net income exceeding $1,500 must file
- Non-resident trusts with Alabama-derived income must file
- Form 41 (Fiduciary Income Tax Return)

## Medicaid Planning (Alabama Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings
- Prepaid burial

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $7,500-8,500/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
- **Unlimited** homestead exemption for head of household up to 160 acres
- Protects against most creditors (not federal tax liens or mortgages)
- Must be primary residence

## Spousal Elective Share
**Statutory Basis**: Alabama Code § 43-8-70, Article 4

### Key Features
- Alabama rejected UPC's "augmented estate" methodology
- Surviving spouse entitled to **lesser of**:
  1. One-third (1/3) of probate estate, OR
  2. Entire estate value MINUS spouse's "separate estate"
- If surviving spouse has larger estate, elective share = $0

### "Separate Estate" Definition
- ALL property owned by surviving spouse after decedent's death
- Includes nonprobate transfers: life insurance, survivorship property, POD accounts, trust distributions

### Applicability
- Only applies when decedent had a Will (not intestacy)
- Only applies if decedent domiciled in Alabama at death
- **Time limit**: Petition within 6 months of later of death or will probate

### Additional Spousal Protections
- Homestead allowance: $15,000 (separate from elective share)
- Exempt property allowance
- Family allowance

### Waiver
- Can be waived by signed writing (prenuptial or postnuptial agreement)

## Probate Procedures

### Small Estate Summary Distribution (Ala. Code § 43-2-692)
**Revised Alabama Small Estates Act (Effective October 1, 2025)**

- **NEW threshold (2025)**: Approximately **$47,000**
- Previous threshold (2024): $36,030
- **Adjustment mechanism**: Threshold adjusted annually based on CPI (Ala. Code § 43-8-116)
- Adjustment effective March 1 each year

### Small Estate Qualifications
- Applies **ONLY to personal property** (real estate excluded)
- No personal representative petition pending or granted
- Minimum 30 days must elapse since filing petition
- Funeral expenses paid or arrangements made
- Debts paid or arrangements made

### Small Estate Process
- No personal representative appointed
- Court orders direct distribution to beneficiaries
- **Notice period**: 30 days (one newspaper publication)
- **Timeline**: Typically 2 months to complete

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from probate court required
- Bank may permit limited examination with death certificate to locate will
- Probate judge in county of residence handles estate administration

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate court involvement

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate planning attorney
- Inform personal representative of box location
- Consider naming executor as authorized signer

## Unique State Features
- Simplified small estate procedures recently expanded
- Independent administration available
- No state income tax on inherited assets
- Traditional dower/curtesy-style elective share (unusual approach)
- Property is **separate property state** (not community property)
- Can disinherit anyone EXCEPT spouse (who has elective share rights)
