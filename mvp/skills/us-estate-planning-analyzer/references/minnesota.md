# Minnesota

## Key Differences from Other States
- **State estate tax** with $3M exemption
- **State gift tax** (limited - 3-year look-back)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- No portability for state estate tax
- Strong homestead protection ($480,000 or 160 acres)
- Transfer on Death Deed available

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Minnesota Statutes Chapter 524 (Uniform Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 524.2-502)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be signed by at least **2 witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign within reasonable time after witnessing

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Minn. Stat. § 524.2-504)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (Minn. Stat. § 524.2-502)
- **Recognized** if material provisions in testator's handwriting and signed
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness does not invalidate will
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Minn. Stat. Chapter 501C (Minnesota Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Minn. Stat. § 501C.0402)
- Minnesota Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Minnesota Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (Minn. Stat. § 501C.0602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (Minn. Stat. § 501C.0808)
- Minnesota allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (Minn. Stat. § 501C.0818)
- Authorized under Minnesota Trust Code
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Requires trustee discretionary power
- Notice requirements apply

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Minn. Stat. Chapter 523 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 523.23)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Statutory form available

### Durability (Minn. Stat. § 523.07)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (Minn. Stat. § 523.23)
- Minnesota provides statutory short form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (Minn. Stat. § 523.24)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Minn. Stat. Chapter 145C (Health Care Directive Act)

### Types Available
- Health Care Directive (combined form)
- Living Will
- Healthcare Agent Appointment

### Execution Requirements (Minn. Stat. § 145C.03)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** OR **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Healthcare provider
  - Employee of healthcare provider where patient is

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Minnesota POLST
- Available in Minnesota
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (Minn. Stat. § 145C.07)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Other relative or close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax

### Minnesota Estate Tax
- **State estate tax applies**
- **Exemption**: $3,000,000 (not indexed)
- **No portability** of exemption between spouses
- **Top rate**: 16%
- Graduated rates from 13% to 16%

### Rate Structure (Minn. Stat. § 291.03)
| Taxable Estate Over Exemption | Rate |
|-------------------------------|------|
| $0 - $200,000 | 13% |
| $200,001 - $700,000 | 14% |
| $700,001 - $1,200,000 | 15% |
| Over $1,200,000 | 16% |

### Filing Requirements
- Return due 9 months after death
- Extension available
- File with Minnesota Department of Revenue
- Must file if gross estate exceeds exemption

### Minnesota Gift Tax
- **Gifts within 3 years of death may be added back**
- Not a separate gift tax but inclusion rule
- Planning timing of gifts important
- Consider 3-year look-back in planning

### No Inheritance Tax
- **No state inheritance tax**
- Federal estate tax applies if over federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million

### Planning Implications
- $3M threshold lower than federal
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- No portability - both spouses must plan
- 3-year gift look-back affects timing
- Consider lifetime gifting (but account for add-back)

## Medicaid Planning (Minnesota Medical Assistance)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: **$3,000** (higher than most states)
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Minnesota-Specific Programs
- **Minnesota Medical Assistance** is Medicaid program
- **Elderly Waiver** for home-based services
- **Alternative Care** program
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $9,000-10,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Minn. Stat. § 510.01 et seq.**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$480,000** in equity protected
- OR **160 acres** in rural areas (regardless of value)
- One of stronger protections in nation

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be owned by debtor
- Automatic protection (but declaration recommended)
- Only one homestead per family

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Agricultural Protection
- 160-acre rural exemption protects family farms
- No value limit for rural homestead
- Significant for agricultural planning

## Transfer on Death Deed
**Minn. Stat. § 507.071**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Must be **recorded before death** with county recorder
- Must designate grantee-beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for Minnesota real property

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (Minn. Stat. § 524.3-301)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Personal representative appointed by registrar
- Most common form

### Formal Probate (Minn. Stat. § 524.3-401)
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (Minn. Stat. § 524.3-1201)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$75,000** in personal property
- 30-day waiting period after death
- No real property included
- Court filing not required

### Summary Administration
- For small estates with real property
- Simplified court process
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from notice

### Court Jurisdiction
- **District Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Gift Tax Look-Back
- Gifts within 3 years of death added to estate
- Not separate gift tax but inclusion rule
- Affects timing of gift planning
- Plan gifts more than 3 years before anticipated death

### Strong Homestead
- $480,000 urban or 160 acres rural
- One of stronger protections nationally
- Agricultural families particularly protected

### Higher Medicaid Asset Limit
- $3,000 individual limit more generous than most states
- Provides more flexibility in planning

### No Portability
- Both spouses must use their exemptions
- Credit shelter trusts essential for married couples
- Cannot rely on surviving spouse election

### Planning Considerations
- Credit shelter trusts essential (no portability)
- Account for 3-year gift look-back in planning
- Strong homestead protection valuable
- Consider TOD deed for real property
- Higher Medicaid asset limit provides flexibility
- Plan gifts more than 3 years in advance

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from district court required
- Minnesota allows examination with death certificate to locate will
- Personal representative needed for full access

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate involvement

### Estate Tax Considerations
- Minnesota has estate tax ($3M threshold)
- Document valuable contents for tax purposes

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document contents for estate tax reporting
- Inform personal representative of box location
