# Virginia

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax (repealed 2007)
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Elective share for surviving spouse
- Holographic wills recognized
- Commissioner of accounts oversees estates
- DC/MD/VA tri-state planning important

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Virginia Code Title 64.2

### Execution Requirements (Va. Code § 64.2-403)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by mark/another at direction)
- Must be signed by at least **2 competent witnesses**
- Witnesses must sign in testator's presence

### Self-Proving Affidavit (Va. Code § 64.2-452)
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate

### Holographic Wills (Va. Code § 64.2-403)
- **Recognized** in Virginia
- Must be **entirely in testator's handwriting**
- Must be **signed by testator**
- No witness requirement
- Date recommended but not required

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Must sign in testator's presence
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Interested witness does not automatically invalidate

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: Virginia Code Title 64.2, Subtitle III (Virginia Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (Va. Code § 64.2-720)
- Virginia Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Virginia UTC governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to beneficiaries required
- Notice upon settlor's death

### Revocable Trusts (Va. Code § 64.2-747)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death

### Directed Trusts (Va. Code § 64.2-770)
- Virginia allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions

### Trust Decanting (Va. Code § 64.2-779.1)
- Authorized under Virginia law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Trustee discretionary power required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: Virginia Code Title 64.2, Chapter 16 (Uniform Power of Attorney Act)

### Execution Requirements (Va. Code § 64.2-1603)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Statutory form available

### Durability (Va. Code § 64.2-1602)
- Durable by default unless document states otherwise
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (Va. Code § 64.2-1612)
- Virginia provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties must accept valid POA

### Agent Duties
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers (Va. Code § 64.2-1622)
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Create, amend, revoke trusts
  - Make gifts
  - Create/change beneficiary designations
  - Create/change rights of survivorship

### Third Party Acceptance (Va. Code § 64.2-1634)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: Virginia Code Title 54.1, Chapter 29 (Health Care Decisions Act)

### Types Available
- Advance Medical Directive (combined form)
- Living Will (Part of directive)
- Healthcare Agent (Part of directive)

### Execution Requirements (Va. Code § 54.1-2983)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Attending physician
  - Related to declarant
  - Named as agent

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Virginia POST
- Available in Virginia
- Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings

### Default Surrogate (Va. Code § 54.1-2986)
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Any adult relative or close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (repealed July 1, 2007)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

### Planning Implications
- No state death taxes simplifies planning
- Focus on federal estate tax for large estates
- Probate avoidance for privacy and efficiency
- DC/MD/VA tri-state considerations important

## Elective Share
**Va. Code § 64.2-302**

### Surviving Spouse Rights
- Surviving spouse can elect against will
- Elective share varies based on children

### Elective Share Calculation
- With children (decedent's): 1/3 of augmented estate
- Without children (or children are both spouses'): 1/2 of augmented estate
- Cannot completely disinherit spouse

### Augmented Estate
- Includes probate estate
- Certain non-probate transfers
- Property owned by surviving spouse

### Waiver
- Can be waived by prenuptial/postnuptial agreement
- Must be in writing and signed

## Medicaid Planning (Virginia Medicaid)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Virginia-Specific Programs
- **Virginia Medicaid** administered by DMAS
- **Commonwealth Coordinated Care Plus** waiver
- **PACE** (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly)
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $8,000-9,000/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Va. Code § 34-4**

### Exemption Amount
- Up to **$25,000** in equity protected (very limited)
- Plus $500 per dependent
- Veterans may have additional exemption

### Requirements
- Must be **head of household**
- Must be primary residence
- Declaration required (file with circuit court clerk)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens
  - Child support/alimony

### Very Limited Protection
- $25,000 is low by national standards
- Consider alternative asset protection strategies
- Trust planning important
- LLC/entity structures for real estate

## Transfer on Death Deed
**Va. Code § 64.2-621 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death **without probate**
- Available since 2013
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime

### Requirements
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **notarized** (acknowledged)
- Must be **recorded before death** with circuit court clerk
- Must designate beneficiary

### Advantages
- Avoids probate for real property
- Simple and cost-effective
- Step-up in basis at death
- Revocable during lifetime

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for Virginia real property

### Revocation
- Record revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will

## Probate Procedures

### Regular Administration (Va. Code § 64.2-500 et seq.)
- Circuit court handles probate
- Executor/administrator appointed
- Inventory and accounting required
- Bond typically required
- Commissioner of accounts oversees

### Commissioner of Accounts
- Unique Virginia feature
- Oversees estate administration
- Reviews accountings
- Ensures proper administration

### Small Estate Procedures (Va. Code § 64.2-601)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$50,000** in personal property
- No real property included
- 60-day waiting period after death

### Timeline
- Regular administration: 6-12 months typical
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Complex estates: 12-24 months
- Creditor claim period: 1 year from qualification

### Court Jurisdiction
- **Circuit Court** handles probate (clerk's office)
- County/city where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### DC/MD/VA Tri-State Planning
- Many residents live/work across jurisdictions
- Maryland has estate AND inheritance tax
- DC has estate tax ($4M exemption)
- Virginia has no death taxes
- Domicile election critical for tax planning

### Commissioner of Accounts
- Unique oversight system
- Reviews all estate accountings
- Adds layer of accountability
- Protects beneficiaries

### Very Limited Homestead
- Only $25,000 exemption
- Consider alternative asset protection
- Trust planning important
- Entity structures for real estate

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Available since 2013
- Popular probate avoidance tool
- Simple and effective

### Planning Considerations
- No state death taxes is major advantage
- Very limited homestead requires planning
- DC/MD/VA domicile election important
- Commissioner oversight is unique
- TOD deeds widely used
- Elective share protects surviving spouse

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary from circuit court required
- Bank may permit limited examination with death certificate
- Commissioner of accounts oversees estate administration

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter retains full access
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without court involvement
- Common for DC-area residents with Virginia accounts

### Tri-State Considerations (DC/MD/VA)
- If assets in multiple jurisdictions, coordinate access
- Virginia administration may be needed for VA safe deposit contents
- Consider domicile implications for multi-state residents

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate planning documents
- Inform executor/administrator of box location
- For multi-state residents, consider consolidating accounts

## State Charitable Incentives

### Neighborhood Assistance Program (NAP) Tax Credit
- **65% credit** for donations to approved organizations serving low-income communities
- Individual and business credits available
- **Among highest credit rates in nation**
- Pre-approval and allocation required

### Land Preservation Tax Credit
- Credit for donated conservation easements
- **40% of fair market value** of easement
- Can be carried forward or **transferred/sold**
- Very valuable for large land holdings
- Transferable credits add liquidity

### Education Improvement Scholarships Tax Credit
- **65% credit** for donations to scholarship foundations
- Supports private school scholarships
- Individual and business credits

### Historic Rehabilitation Tax Credit
- Up to **25% credit** for rehabilitation of historic structures
- Can apply to preservation easement donations
- Valuable for historic property owners

### Estate Planning Implications
- NAP 65% credit is **among highest in nation** for qualified donations
- Land preservation credit extremely valuable for landowners
- Credits can be **transferred/sold** - adds liquidity
- DC/MD/VA tri-state planning important (MD has estate + inheritance tax)
- Consider Virginia domicile for death tax planning vs. MD/DC
- Coordinate charitable giving across jurisdictions
- Very limited homestead means other asset protection needed
