# Kansas

## Key Differences from Other States
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Common law property state
- **Unlimited rural homestead protection** (160 acres rural, 1 acre urban)
- Transfer on Death Deed available
- Uniform Probate Code adopted
- Simple probate procedures

## Will Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: K.S.A. Chapter 59 (Probate Code)

### Execution Requirements (K.S.A. 59-606)
- Testator must be **18+ years old**
- Must be of **sound mind**
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by testator** (or by another at direction in presence)
- Must be **attested and subscribed** by **2 or more competent witnesses** in presence of testator

### Self-Proving Affidavit (K.S.A. 59-606(c))
- Available and recommended
- Testator and witnesses sign affidavit before notary
- Creates presumption of proper execution
- Avoids need to locate witnesses at probate
- Can be executed at same time as will or later

### Holographic Wills
- **NOT recognized** in Kansas
- All wills must be witnessed
- Foreign holographic wills may be recognized if valid where executed

### Witness Requirements
- Must be competent witnesses
- Interested witness allowed but may affect gift
- Should not be beneficiaries (best practice)
- Must sign in presence of testator

### Foreign Wills (K.S.A. 59-601)
- Valid if executed in compliance with:
  - Kansas law, OR
  - Law of state/country where executed, OR
  - Law of testator's domicile at execution or death

## Trust Requirements
**Statutory Basis**: K.S.A. Chapter 58a (Kansas Uniform Trust Code)

### Trust Creation (K.S.A. 58a-402)
- Kansas Uniform Trust Code adopted
- Must have trust property
- Must have ascertainable beneficiaries (or valid purpose)
- Settlor must have capacity
- Writing required for real property

### Trust Administration
- Kansas Uniform Trust Code governs
- Prudent Investor Rule applies
- Trustee has duties of loyalty, impartiality
- Accounting to qualified beneficiaries required
- Notice requirements upon death

### Revocable Trusts (K.S.A. 58a-602)
- Common for probate avoidance
- Settlor retains control during lifetime
- Presumed revocable unless stated otherwise
- Becomes irrevocable at settlor's death
- Creditor claims apply

### Directed Trusts (K.S.A. 58a-808)
- Kansas allows directed trusts
- Trust advisor can direct trustee
- Trustee liability limited when following directions
- Useful for investment management

### Trust Decanting (K.S.A. 58a-818)
- Authorized under Kansas law
- Allows modification of irrevocable trusts
- Requires trustee discretionary power
- Notice to beneficiaries required

## Power of Attorney
**Statutory Basis**: K.S.A. 58-650 et seq.

### Execution Requirements (K.S.A. 58-652)
- Must be **signed by principal** (or at direction)
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Witnesses recommended but not required
- Must clearly identify agent

### Durability (K.S.A. 58-651)
- Durable if includes durability language
- Must state: "This power of attorney shall not be affected by subsequent disability or incapacity of the principal"
- Non-durable POA terminates upon incapacity
- Springing POA available

### Statutory Form (K.S.A. 58-654)
- Kansas provides statutory form (recommended)
- Other forms valid if properly executed
- Third parties should accept valid POA

### Agent Duties (K.S.A. 58-656)
- Fiduciary duty to principal
- Must act in principal's best interest
- Must keep records
- Must avoid conflicts of interest

### Hot Powers
- Certain powers require express grant:
  - Make gifts
  - Create or modify trusts
  - Change beneficiary designations
  - Delegate authority

### Third Party Acceptance (K.S.A. 58-658)
- Third parties must accept valid POA
- May request certification
- Refusal may result in liability
- Good faith reliance protected

## Healthcare Directive
**Statutory Basis**: K.S.A. 65-28,101 et seq. (Kansas Natural Death Act); K.S.A. 58-625 et seq. (Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care)

### Types Available
- Declaration (Living Will) - K.S.A. 65-28,103
- Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care - K.S.A. 58-629
- Combined Advance Directive

### Living Will Requirements (K.S.A. 65-28,103)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by declarant** (or at direction)
- Must be **witnessed by 2 adults** AND **notarized**
- Witnesses cannot be:
  - Related by blood or marriage
  - Entitled to any portion of estate
  - Responsible for healthcare costs
- One witness cannot be attending physician or employee

### Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care (K.S.A. 58-629)
- Must be **in writing**
- Must be **signed by principal**
- Must be **notarized**
- Must be witnessed by **2 adults** who are not:
  - Related to principal
  - Entitled to estate
  - Healthcare provider

### Agent Authority
- Make healthcare decisions when principal incapacitated
- Access medical records
- Consent to or refuse treatment
- Arrange care and placement
- Make anatomical gift decisions

### Kansas POLST (K.S.A. 65-4970)
- Available in Kansas
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Completed with healthcare provider
- Portable across healthcare settings
- Bright pink form

### Default Surrogate
- If no healthcare directive:
  - Guardian
  - Spouse or domestic partner
  - Adult child
  - Parent
  - Adult sibling
  - Other relative or close friend

## Estate and Inheritance Tax
- **No state estate tax** (repealed 1998)
- **No state inheritance tax**
- **No state gift tax**
- Only federal estate tax applies if estate exceeds federal threshold
- 2025 federal exemption: $13.99 million per person

## Medicaid Planning (KanCare)

### Look-Back Period
- **60 months** (5 years)

### Asset Limits (2025)
- Individual: $2,000
- Married (community spouse): CSRA up to federal maximum (~$154,140)
- CSRA minimum: approximately $31,584

### Income Limits
- Approximately $2,829/month for individuals
- Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) available if over limit

### Exempt Assets
- Primary residence (equity limit ~$713,000)
- One vehicle
- Personal belongings and household goods
- Prepaid irrevocable burial contracts
- Term life insurance
- Whole life with face value under $1,500

### Kansas-Specific Programs
- **KanCare** is Kansas Medicaid managed care program
- **HCBS (Home and Community Based Services) Waiver**
- **Frail Elderly (FE) Waiver**
- **PACE** programs in some areas
- Estate recovery program active

### Spousal Protections
- Community spouse resource allowance (CSRA)
- Minimum monthly maintenance needs allowance (MMMNA)
- Court order for increased CSRA available

### Penalty Divisor
- Approximately $6,500-7,500/month (verify current rate)

## Homestead Protection
**Kansas Constitution Article 15, Section 9; K.S.A. 60-2301**

### Exemption Amount - Constitutional Protection
- **Rural**: Up to **160 acres** (unlimited value)
- **Urban**: Up to **1 acre** (unlimited value)
- No dollar limit on value
- Constitutional guarantee

### Requirements
- Must be **principal residence**
- Must be occupied by family
- Only one homestead per family
- Automatic protection (no filing required)

### Scope of Protection
- Protected from most judgment creditors
- Constitutional protection (cannot be waived except by specific means)
- Does NOT protect against:
  - Mortgages and deeds of trust
  - Property tax liens
  - Mechanic's liens (for work on homestead)
  - Purchase money obligations
  - Federal tax liens

### Surviving Spouse Rights
- Descends to surviving spouse
- Protected for life of surviving spouse
- Strong constitutional protection

## Transfer on Death Deed
**K.S.A. 59-3501 et seq.**

### Features
- Transfer real property on death without probate
- Revocable during owner's lifetime
- Does not affect owner's rights during lifetime
- Simple alternative to trust for real property

### Requirements
- Must be in **writing**
- Must be **signed** by owner
- Must be **acknowledged (notarized)**
- Must be **recorded before death** with register of deeds
- Must contain statutory language
- Must designate grantee-beneficiary

### Limitations
- Subject to decedent's debts
- Medicaid estate recovery applies
- Subject to claims of creditors
- Only for Kansas real property

### Revocation
- Can revoke by recording revocation instrument
- Subsequent TOD deed supersedes prior
- Conveyance during life revokes
- Not revoked by will
- Marriage does not revoke

## Probate Procedures

### Informal Probate (K.S.A. 59-2287a)
- Available for uncontested estates
- Minimal court involvement
- Based on Uniform Probate Code
- Most common form

### Formal Probate
- Required if will contested
- Full court supervision
- More expensive and time-consuming

### Small Estate Procedures (K.S.A. 59-1507b)
- **Affidavit procedure** for estates under **$40,000**
- 30-day waiting period after death
- Personal property only (no real property)
- Simplified transfer procedure

### Simplified Administration (K.S.A. 59-3501)
- For estates where all property passes to surviving spouse
- Simplified procedures
- Faster distribution

### Timeline
- Informal probate: 6-12 months typical
- Formal probate: 12-24 months
- Small estate: 2-4 months
- Creditor claim period: 4 months from first publication

### Court Jurisdiction
- **District Court** handles probate
- County where decedent resided has jurisdiction

## Unique State Features

### Constitutional Homestead Protection
- 160 acres rural / 1 acre urban
- No dollar limit
- One of strongest in nation
- Cannot be waived except by mortgage or sale

### No Death Taxes
- No state estate tax
- No state inheritance tax
- Simplified estate administration
- Only federal considerations

### Uniform Probate Code
- Informal probate available
- Efficient estate administration
- Modern trust code
- Simplified procedures

### Agricultural Considerations
- Farm succession planning important
- 160-acre homestead protects family farms
- Conservation easements common
- Special agricultural provisions

### Planning Considerations
- No state death tax simplifies planning
- TOD deeds effective for real property
- Constitutional homestead provides strong asset protection
- Kansas Uniform Trust Code provides modern administration
- Consider revocable trust for privacy and multi-state property
- Strong homestead protection is significant planning tool

## Safe Deposit Box Rules

### Access After Death
- Joint renter has immediate access
- Without joint renter: letters testamentary required
- Bank may permit examination with death certificate
- Can search for will, burial instructions
- Inventory required for removal

### Joint Ownership
- Surviving joint renter has full access rights
- No automatic freeze upon death
- Contents accessible without probate
- Consider liability exposure

### Best Practices
- Original will should NOT be stored in safe deposit box
- Document box contents with estate plan
- Inform executor of location
- Consider adding fiduciary as deputy
