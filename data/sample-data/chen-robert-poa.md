# DURABLE POWER OF ATTORNEY

## Robert James Chen

---

## NOTICE TO PRINCIPAL

**IMPORTANT INFORMATION**

This is an important legal document. Before signing this document, you should know these important facts. By signing this document, you are not giving up any powers or rights to control your finances and property yourself. In addition to any powers you may give your agent under this document, you can continue to make your own decisions about your finances and property as long as you are able to do so.

This document gives the person you designate as your agent the power to make decisions about your finances and property. Your agent can make these decisions only when you cannot make them yourself, or when you specifically authorize your agent to act.

## ARTICLE I: DESIGNATION OF AGENT

### Section 1.1 — Primary Agent

I appoint **SUSAN MARIE CHEN**, my spouse, currently residing at 847 Maple Ridge Drive, Lexington, MA 02421, as my Attorney-in-Fact and Agent (hereinafter "Agent").

### Section 1.2 — Successor Agent

If Susan Marie Chen is unable or unwilling to act as my Agent, I appoint **DAVID WILLIAM CHEN**, my brother, currently residing at 512 Oak Street, Newton, MA 02460, as my Successor Agent.

### Section 1.3 — Agent Acceptance

Any Agent named herein who accepts this appointment agrees to act in my best interest and in accordance with the terms of this document and applicable Massachusetts law.

---

## ARTICLE II: GRANT OF AUTHORITY

### Section 2.1 — General Grant

I grant to my Agent full power and authority to act on my behalf in any and all financial and property matters, as fully and effectively as I might or could do if personally present, including but not limited to the powers enumerated below.

### Section 2.2 — Specific Powers

My Agent shall have the following specific powers:

**(a) Real Property Transactions.** To acquire, sell, exchange, lease, mortgage, and in any manner deal with real property and any interest therein, including my homestead;

**(b) Personal Property Transactions.** To acquire, sell, exchange, and otherwise deal with tangible personal property, including but not limited to goods, motor vehicles, furnishings, and other personal effects;

**(c) Banking and Financial Transactions.** To make deposits to, withdrawals from, and manage all bank, savings, credit union, and other financial accounts; to negotiate, endorse, and deposit checks and other negotiable instruments;

**(d) Investment Transactions.** To buy, sell, exchange, and otherwise manage stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and other securities and investments; to establish and manage brokerage accounts; to exercise stock options and rights;

**(e) Insurance and Annuity Transactions.** To obtain, manage, maintain, or terminate insurance policies and annuity contracts; to pay premiums; to submit claims; to receive proceeds;

**(f) Retirement Plan Transactions.** To manage retirement plan accounts, including 401(k), IRA, pension, and other retirement plans; to make contributions, withdrawals, and rollovers; to change beneficiary designations;

**(g) Tax Matters.** To prepare, sign, and file federal, state, and local tax returns and extensions; to represent me before taxing authorities; to pay taxes owed; to receive and manage tax refunds;

**(h) Business Operations.** To manage, operate, and make decisions regarding any business interests I may own, including but not limited to stock options and equity compensation;

**(i) Legal and Claims.** To commence, prosecute, discontinue, or defend any legal proceeding; to settle, compromise, or submit to arbitration any claim or dispute;

**(j) Government Benefits.** To apply for and manage government benefits and entitlements on my behalf, including Social Security, Medicare, and veterans' benefits;

**(k) Estate Planning.** To create, amend, or revoke trusts; to make gifts consistent with my established patterns of giving; to fund trusts; to disclaim interests; provided that such actions are consistent with my overall estate plan;

**(l) Personal and Family Maintenance.** To provide for the support, maintenance, education, and care of myself, my spouse, and my minor children from my assets;

**(m) Record Keeping.** To maintain records and accounts of all transactions made on my behalf.

---

## ARTICLE III: DURABILITY AND EFFECTIVE DATE

### Section 3.1 — Durable Power

This Power of Attorney shall be durable. **This Power of Attorney shall not be affected by my subsequent disability or incapacity.** This Power of Attorney shall remain in full force and effect until revoked by me in writing, or until my death.

### Section 3.2 — Effective Date

This Power of Attorney shall become effective immediately upon execution.

### Section 3.3 — Incapacity

If I become incapacitated, my Agent's authority under this document shall continue uninterrupted. Incapacity shall be determined by two licensed physicians who have personally examined me and provided written statements that I am unable to manage my financial affairs.

---

## ARTICLE IV: THIRD-PARTY RELIANCE

### Section 4.1 — Good Faith Reliance

Any third party who receives a copy of this Power of Attorney may act under it. Any third party may rely upon the representations of my Agent as to any matter relating to this Power of Attorney or the authority granted herein.

### Section 4.2 — Indemnification

I agree to indemnify and hold harmless any third party who acts in good faith reliance upon this Power of Attorney.

---

## ARTICLE V: AGENT'S DUTIES AND LIMITATIONS

### Section 5.1 — Fiduciary Duty

My Agent shall act in my best interest, exercise reasonable care, and keep accurate records of all transactions conducted on my behalf.

### Section 5.2 — Compensation

My Agent shall serve without compensation, except for reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in carrying out duties under this Power of Attorney.

### Section 5.3 — Bond

No bond shall be required of any Agent named herein.

### Section 5.4 — Accounting

My Agent shall maintain a written record of all receipts, disbursements, and significant transactions made on my behalf, and shall make such records available to me or my legal representative upon request.

---

## ARTICLE VI: REVOCATION

### Section 6.1 — Right to Revoke

I reserve the right to revoke this Power of Attorney at any time by written notice to my Agent. Revocation shall be effective upon delivery of notice to my Agent.

### Section 6.2 — Effect of Divorce

If my marriage to Susan Marie Chen is dissolved by divorce or annulment, her appointment as my Agent under this Power of Attorney shall be automatically revoked, and the Successor Agent shall assume all powers granted herein.

---

## ARTICLE VII: GOVERNING LAW

This Power of Attorney shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, including the Massachusetts Uniform Power of Attorney Act, M.G.L. c. 190B, Article V.
