# LAST WILL AND TESTAMENT OF ROBERT JAMES CHEN

## Pour-Over Will

---

I, **ROBERT JAMES CHEN**, a resident of Lexington, Middlesex County, Commonwealth of Massachusetts, being of sound mind and memory, and not acting under duress or undue influence, do hereby make, publish, and declare this to be my Last Will and Testament, hereby revoking all previous wills and codicils.

---

## ARTICLE I: IDENTIFICATION

### Section 1.1 — Personal Information

I am married to **SUSAN MARIE CHEN**. I have two (2) children:

1. **EMILY CHEN**, born September 10, 2006
2. **MICHAEL CHEN**, born November 28, 2009

### Section 1.2 — Existing Trust

I have established the **CHEN FAMILY REVOCABLE LIVING TRUST** dated March 15, 2016 (hereinafter "the Trust"), of which I am a Co-Grantor and Co-Trustee.

---

## ARTICLE II: PAYMENT OF DEBTS AND EXPENSES

### Section 2.1 — Debts

I direct my Executor to pay from my probate estate all of my legally enforceable debts, funeral expenses, and costs of last illness as soon as practicable after my death.

### Section 2.2 — Taxes

I direct that all estate, inheritance, succession, and similar taxes assessed by reason of my death shall be paid from the Trust Estate as provided in the Trust Agreement, unless the Trust does not contain sufficient assets, in which case such taxes shall be paid from my probate estate.

---

## ARTICLE III: POUR-OVER PROVISION

### Section 3.1 — Residuary Estate

I give, devise, and bequeath all of the rest, residue, and remainder of my property, of every kind and description, wherever situated, whether real, personal, or mixed, including any property over which I may have a power of appointment, to the then-acting Trustee or Trustees of the **CHEN FAMILY REVOCABLE LIVING TRUST** dated March 15, 2016, as amended, to be held, administered, and distributed in accordance with the terms of said Trust Agreement, including any amendments made thereto before or after the execution of this Will.

### Section 3.2 — Trust Incorporation

If for any reason the Trust is not in existence at the time of my death, or if the Trust is held to be invalid or unenforceable, then I direct that my residuary estate be distributed as if the Trust were in effect and I had died as the surviving Grantor under the Trust, applying the distribution provisions of the Trust as they existed at the time of my death.

---

## ARTICLE IV: EXECUTOR

### Section 4.1 — Appointment of Executor

I hereby nominate and appoint my wife, **SUSAN MARIE CHEN**, as the Executor of this Will.

### Section 4.2 — Successor Executor

If Susan Marie Chen is unable or unwilling to serve as Executor, I nominate and appoint my brother, **DAVID WILLIAM CHEN**, of Newton, Massachusetts, as Successor Executor.

### Section 4.3 — Executor Powers

My Executor shall have, in addition to all powers granted by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, the following powers:

(a) To sell, at public or private sale, any property belonging to my estate, with or without notice, and upon such terms as my Executor deems advisable;

(b) To compromise, settle, or abandon any claims in favor of or against my estate;

(c) To employ attorneys, accountants, and other professional advisors as my Executor deems necessary;

(d) To borrow money for the purposes of estate administration;

(e) To distribute assets in kind, in cash, or partly in each, at values determined by my Executor in good faith.

### Section 4.4 — Bond

No bond or surety shall be required of any Executor named herein.

---

## ARTICLE V: GUARDIAN

### Section 5.1 — Guardian of Minor Children

If my spouse, Susan Marie Chen, does not survive me, I nominate and appoint **JENNIFER CHEN**, my sister, currently residing in Cambridge, Massachusetts, as guardian of the person of any of my minor children.

### Section 5.2 — Successor Guardian

If Jennifer Chen is unable or unwilling to serve as guardian, I nominate and appoint **MARGARET LIU**, a family friend currently residing in Brookline, Massachusetts, as successor guardian.

### Section 5.3 — Guardian's Authority

The guardian shall have full authority over the care, custody, education, and upbringing of my minor children, and shall exercise such authority in the best interests of the children.

---

## ARTICLE VI: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 6.1 — Simultaneous Death

If my spouse and I die simultaneously, or under circumstances making it uncertain which of us survived the other, I shall be deemed to have predeceased my spouse for purposes of this Will.

### Section 6.2 — Survivorship Requirement

No person shall be deemed to have survived me unless such person survives me by thirty (30) days. If a beneficiary fails to survive me by thirty days, this Will shall take effect as though such beneficiary had predeceased me.

### Section 6.3 — Severability

If any provision of this Will is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 6.4 — Governing Law

This Will shall be construed and administered according to the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
