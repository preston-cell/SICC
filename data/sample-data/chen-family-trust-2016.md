# CHEN FAMILY REVOCABLE LIVING TRUST

## Trust Agreement

**Date of Execution:** March 15, 2016

**Jurisdiction:** Commonwealth of Massachusetts

---

## ARTICLE I: CREATION OF TRUST

### Section 1.1 — Declaration of Trust

ROBERT JAMES CHEN and SUSAN MARIE CHEN (hereinafter referred to as "Grantors" or "Trustees"), residents of Middlesex County, Commonwealth of Massachusetts, hereby declare that they hold the property described in Schedule A attached hereto, in trust, for the uses and purposes and upon the terms and conditions set forth in this Trust Agreement.

### Section 1.2 — Name of Trust

This Trust shall be known as the **"CHEN FAMILY REVOCABLE LIVING TRUST"** dated March 15, 2016.

### Section 1.3 — Revocability

This Trust is revocable and may be amended, modified, or revoked in whole or in part by either Grantor during their lifetime, in accordance with Article IX of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE II: TRUST PROPERTY

### Section 2.1 — Initial Trust Property

The Grantors hereby transfer and convey to the Trustees the property described in Schedule A, receipt of which is hereby acknowledged by the Trustees. This property, together with any additional property that may be transferred to this Trust, shall constitute the **Trust Estate**.

### Section 2.2 — Additional Contributions

The Grantors, or any other person, may at any time transfer additional property to the Trust Estate by deed, assignment, or other conveyance, and such property shall be held, administered, and distributed in accordance with the terms of this Trust Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE III: TRUSTEES

### Section 3.1 — Initial Trustees

ROBERT JAMES CHEN and SUSAN MARIE CHEN shall serve as the initial Co-Trustees of this Trust.

### Section 3.2 — Successor Trustee

In the event that both Co-Trustees are unable or unwilling to serve as Trustees, by reason of death, incapacity, or resignation, then **DAVID WILLIAM CHEN**, brother of Robert James Chen, currently residing in Newton, Massachusetts, shall serve as Successor Trustee.

### Section 3.3 — Trustee Powers

The Trustees shall have all powers granted by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, including but not limited to the following powers, to be exercised in a fiduciary capacity:

(a) To sell, exchange, lease, or otherwise dispose of any property held in trust;

(b) To invest and reinvest trust funds in any property or investment, including but not limited to stocks, bonds, mutual funds, real estate, and any other investment the Trustees deem prudent;

(c) To borrow money, pledge trust property, and mortgage or encumber trust assets as security for obligations;

(d) To employ attorneys, accountants, investment advisors, and other agents as the Trustees deem necessary;

(e) To make distributions as provided in this Trust Agreement;

(f) To execute and deliver any instruments necessary to carry out the purposes of this Trust;

(g) To manage and operate any business interest held in trust;

(h) To deal with any fiduciary, including the Grantors in their individual capacity;

(i) To compromise, settle, or abandon claims or disputes;

(j) To establish reserves for anticipated expenses or liabilities.

### Section 3.4 — Compensation

The initial Co-Trustees shall serve without compensation. Any successor Trustee who is not a beneficiary of this Trust shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered.

### Section 3.5 — Bond

No Trustee named herein shall be required to furnish any bond or surety.

---

## ARTICLE IV: ADMINISTRATION DURING GRANTORS' LIFETIMES

### Section 4.1 — Use of Trust Property

During the lifetimes of both Grantors, the Trustees shall hold, manage, invest, and reinvest the Trust Estate and shall pay to or apply for the benefit of the Grantors all of the net income of the Trust, and such amounts of the principal as the Grantors may request from time to time.

### Section 4.2 — Incapacity of a Grantor

If either Grantor becomes incapacitated as determined by two licensed physicians, the remaining Grantor, as sole Trustee, or the Successor Trustee if both Grantors are incapacitated, shall use the Trust income and principal as necessary for the care, comfort, health, and maintenance of the incapacitated Grantor, taking into account the incapacitated Grantor's accustomed standard of living.

### Section 4.3 — Revocation or Amendment

Either Grantor may, by written instrument delivered to the other Grantor or to the Trustee, amend, revoke, or partially revoke this Trust Agreement. Any amendment or revocation shall be effective upon delivery to the Trustee.

---

## ARTICLE V: DISTRIBUTION UPON DEATH OF FIRST GRANTOR

### Section 5.1 — Surviving Grantor's Share

Upon the death of the first Grantor to die, the Trust Estate shall be divided into the following sub-trusts:

**(a) Survivor's Trust:** The surviving Grantor's share of the Trust Estate, plus any property that does not qualify for the marital deduction, shall be held in a Survivor's Trust for the benefit of the surviving Grantor. The surviving Grantor shall have full power to amend, revoke, or withdraw from the Survivor's Trust.

**(b) Marital Trust:** The deceased Grantor's share shall be held in a Marital Trust for the benefit of the surviving Grantor. The Trustee shall distribute all income from the Marital Trust to the surviving Grantor at least quarterly, and shall distribute principal as reasonably necessary for the surviving Grantor's health, education, maintenance, and support.

### Section 5.2 — Surviving Grantor as Sole Trustee

Upon the death of the first Grantor, the surviving Grantor shall serve as sole Trustee of both the Survivor's Trust and the Marital Trust, with the Successor Trustee named in Section 3.2 serving in the event of the surviving Grantor's death, incapacity, or resignation.

---

## ARTICLE VI: DISTRIBUTION UPON DEATH OF SURVIVING GRANTOR

### Section 6.1 — Final Distribution

Upon the death of the surviving Grantor (or upon the death of both Grantors simultaneously), after payment of all debts, expenses, and taxes, the remaining Trust Estate shall be distributed as follows:

**(a)** To the Grantors' children, **EMILY CHEN** and **MICHAEL CHEN**, in equal shares;

**(b)** If either child has predeceased the surviving Grantor, that child's share shall be distributed to that child's issue, per stirpes;

**(c)** If either child has predeceased the surviving Grantor leaving no issue, the deceased child's share shall be distributed to the surviving child or children.

### Section 6.2 — Minor Beneficiary Provisions

If any beneficiary is under the age of twenty-five (25) at the time of distribution, the Trustee shall hold that beneficiary's share in a separate sub-trust and shall distribute to or for the benefit of such beneficiary:

(a) All income and such amounts of principal as the Trustee deems necessary for the beneficiary's health, education, maintenance, and support;

(b) One-third (1/3) of the remaining principal upon the beneficiary reaching age twenty-five (25);

(c) One-half (1/2) of the remaining principal upon the beneficiary reaching age thirty (30);

(d) The remaining balance upon the beneficiary reaching age thirty-five (35).

### Section 6.3 — Spendthrift Provision

No interest in the principal or income of any trust created under this Agreement shall be anticipated, assigned, or encumbered, or be subject to any creditor's claim or to legal process, prior to its actual receipt by the beneficiary.

---

## ARTICLE VII: TAX PROVISIONS

### Section 7.1 — Federal Estate Tax

The Trustee shall pay from the Trust Estate all estate, inheritance, succession, and similar taxes payable by reason of the death of either Grantor. Such taxes shall be apportioned as provided by applicable state law unless the Grantor's Will provides otherwise.

### Section 7.2 — Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax

The Trustee is authorized to allocate the Grantors' available generation-skipping transfer tax exemption to any transfer made under this Trust Agreement in such manner as the Trustee deems advisable.

### Section 7.3 — Income Tax Reporting

During the Grantors' lifetimes, all income earned by the Trust Estate shall be reported on the Grantors' personal income tax returns, as this Trust is a grantor trust for income tax purposes.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 8.1 — Governing Law

This Trust Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

### Section 8.2 — Severability

If any provision of this Trust Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, such invalidity or unenforceability shall not affect the remaining provisions.

### Section 8.3 — Headings

The headings used in this Trust Agreement are for convenience only and shall not be used in construing the provisions hereof.

### Section 8.4 — Entire Agreement

This Trust Agreement, together with any amendments hereto, constitutes the entire agreement with respect to the Trust Estate.

### Section 8.5 — No Contest Clause

If any beneficiary shall, directly or indirectly, contest or attack this Trust Agreement or any of its provisions, any share or interest in the Trust Estate given to that contesting beneficiary is revoked and shall be disposed of as if the contesting beneficiary had predeceased all Grantors without issue.

---

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENT AND REVOCATION

### Section 9.1 — Right to Amend

Either Grantor may amend this Trust Agreement at any time by written instrument signed by the amending Grantor and delivered to the Trustee.

### Section 9.2 — Right to Revoke

Either Grantor may revoke this Trust Agreement in whole or in part at any time by written instrument signed by the revoking Grantor and delivered to the Trustee.

### Section 9.3 — Effect of Revocation

Upon revocation, the Trustee shall deliver the Trust Estate, or the specified portion thereof, to the revoking Grantor free and clear of all trust obligations.

---

## ARTICLE X: MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 10.1 — Counterparts

This Trust Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

### Section 10.2 — Notices

Any notice required or permitted under this Trust Agreement shall be in writing and shall be delivered personally or sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the Trustee at the address of record.
